## Group Narrative
Group B has been defined by two contrasting stories: Canada's
co-host confidence exploding against Qatar, and Switzerland's
late-game ruthlessness dismantling Bosnia.

The Canada 6-0 was the statement result. Jonathan David's hat trick
against Qatar announced that this Golden Generation isn't just
qualifying — they're here to win matches. Jesse Marsch's pressing
system was relentless, Alphonso Davies created space with every
run, and the scoreline reflected genuine dominance rather than
fortunate circumstances.

Switzerland's 4-1 tells a different story about the same quality gap.
After being held by Qatar through a Muheim own goal in MD1, Yakin's
side produced three goals in the final 12 minutes against Bosnia.
Johan Manzambi — 20 years old, born 2005, playing for SC Freiburg —
scored twice as a substitute in what became the individual breakthrough
performance of the group stage.

Bosnia's tournament has been painful. They were the better side for
long periods against Canada but couldn't hold the lead. Against
Switzerland, they were on the verge of a point at 1-1 in the 90th
minute before the collapse. Edin Dzeko at 40 remains a presence but
the team around him lacked the resilience to match their moments of
quality.

Qatar's 90+4' equalizer against Switzerland was their peak — a
moment of fortune that papered over structural limitations. Against
Canada, those limitations were brutally exposed. Lopetegui faces
a longer rebuild than a single World Cup can solve.
