## Matchday 1 Results (June 15)
- FRA 3-1 SEN — Mbappé 66' 90+6', Barcola 82' / Mbaye 90+5'. France in
  control but Senegal made it interesting late. Mbaye's consolation, scored
  at 17 years old in injury time, was the group stage's most emotional
  individual moment. Mbappé sealed it in stoppage time immediately after.
- IRQ 1-4 NOR — Hussein 39' / Haaland 29' 43', Østigård 76', Hussein OG
  90+6'. Haaland brace before half-time. Iraq scored but Norway were
  dominant. The OG from Hussein in the final minute added brutal irony.

## Group Narrative
Group I is the tournament's most star-laden group. France and Norway
arrived with genuine title aspirations. Senegal and Iraq have youth and
story. The Matchday 1 results split cleanly: both heavyweights won, both
underdogs conceded three-plus.

Norway's 28-year return to the World Cup was announced exactly as their
fans dreamed: Haaland scoring twice before the 45-minute mark. The
partnership between Haaland's movement and Ødegaard's service looked
natural and dangerous — two Arsenal/Man City stars combining for the
national team as if the club season hadn't ended. Norway lead the group
on goal difference. The statement was made.

France under Deschamps played the Deschamps way: controlled, patient,
lethal in the second half. Barcola's emergence — scoring from the left
in a role previously locked to established names — suggests Deschamps is
willing to evolve. Mbappé scoring twice, including in the final minute,
confirmed the hierarchy. The captain delivers when the match needs him.

Senegal's youth project is fascinating and fragile. Ibrahim Mbaye, 17
years old, PSG academy product, scored a consolation goal against the
world's best team in the 90th minute of a World Cup match. That is
extraordinary. The squad around him — Lamine Camara, Assane Diao, Mamadou
Sarr — is building toward something. Whether it arrives in time to survive
this group is the question.

Iraq's return after 40 years was marked by a goal from Aymen Hussein and
three goals conceded from Haaland. Graham Arnold brought his 2022
Socceroos methodology across, with Rene Meulensteen's Manchester United
coaching experience in the technical staff. The result showed the quality
gap. The story is larger than the scoreline.
