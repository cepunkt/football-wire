## Matchday 1 Results (June 19)
- POR 1-1 COD — Joao Neves 6' / Wissa 45+5'. Portugal's 21-year-old
  PSG midfielder scored early. DR Congo's Newcastle forward equalized
  in first-half stoppage time. Full time 1-1. A result that serves
  neither Portugal's ambitions nor DR Congo's survival hopes.
- UZB 1-3 COL — Munoz 40' / Fayzullaev 60' / L. Diaz 65' /
  Campaz 90+9'. Colombia led, Uzbekistan equalized through Fayzullaev
  (their first ever World Cup goal), then Luis Diaz and late substitute
  Campaz completed the win. Uzbekistan competed for an hour.

## Group Narrative
The group nobody expected to be complicated and already is. Portugal
dropped points to DR Congo. Colombia look like the class of the
group but let Uzbekistan back in before sealing it late. The draw
everyone assumed — Portugal advancing comfortably — is already under
pressure after one game.

Joao Neves scored after six minutes against DR Congo and Portugal
looked like they'd cruise. Then Wissa's 45+5' equalizer happened.
Whatever Roberto Martinez's system is doing, it isn't converting
possession dominance into goals with enough efficiency. The 41-year-
old Cristiano Ronaldo's role in that equation is the uncomfortable
question. Joao Neves being the one to score while Ronaldo is on the
pitch is the clearest signal of where Portuguese football is headed.

Colombia look right. Luis Diaz from the left, Munoz bombing forward
from right-back, James Rodriguez threading through from the 10
position. When Fayzullaev equalized at 60' and it was 1-1, Colombia
showed composure — Diaz scored five minutes later and Campaz buried
it in the 99th minute. The difference between Copa America finalists
and a team at their first World Cup, distilled into half an hour.

The most remarkable story in Group K is Uzbekistan: Fabio Cannavaro,
2006 World Cup winner, managing a Central Asian nation with his
brother Paolo on the bench. Abdukodir Khusanov, born in Uzbekistan
in 2004, playing Champions League football at Manchester City, anchoring
the defence. Fayzullaev scoring to make it 1-1 against Colombia. A
nation of 36 million people at their first World Cup, and for 25
minutes they had drawn level with one of South America's strongest
teams. The kernel of what might become.
