## Matchday 1 Results (June 19-20)
- ENG 4-2 CRO — Kane 12' (pen 42'), Bellingham 47', Rashford 85' /
  Baturina 36', Musa 45+5'. Croatia fought hard — level at half-time
  after Baturina and Musa replied. England's second half was decisive:
  Bellingham's third, Rashford's seal. A 4-2 that was 2-2 at half-time.

  a 20-year-old who plays in Denmark. Ghana defended, organized,
  waited, and then Caleb Yirenkyi scored with what was almost the
  last touch of the match.

## Group Narrative
England are here. Not cautiously, not carefully — four goals against
Croatia, three different scorers, Tuchel's aggressive system on full
display. The German coach's first tournament impression is emphatic:
this England attacks, it scores, and it doesn't apologize for ambition.
Kane with a brace (the second a penalty), Bellingham threading the
third from midfield, Rashford curling the fourth on the counter. If
this is what Tuchel's England looks like at 4-2 vs Croatia, opponents
will be afraid.

Croatia gave England a genuine scare. Baturina's equalizer at 36' —
the 22-year-old Como midfielder, one of the tournament's young names
to know — and Musa's 45+5' goal to make it 2-2 at the break. Dalic's
team showed character and quality in that first half. The second half
told a harder truth: Modric is 40, Perisic is 37, the legs that made
2018 magic aren't what they were. Bellingham at 22 and Rashford
reborn at Barcelona settled it. The generation gap, on display.

Ghana did exactly what Queiroz teams do. They organized. They
defended. They waited. And then Caleb Yirenkyi — 20 years old, born
January 2006, playing his club football at FC Nordsjaelland in
Denmark — scored in the 95th minute. Three points from a clean
sheet and a last-gasp goal is the most Queiroz result in Queiroz
football history. The 2010 memory of Gyan's penalty haunts Ghanaian
football. This generation writes different stories.

Panama were competitive and got nothing. Fell to a 95th-minute goal
in their opening match of only their second World Cup. Christiansen's
team will need to find goals in the next two matches — against
Croatia in desperation and potentially against England — or the
second World Cup ends the same way as the first: eliminated in the
group stage without a win.
