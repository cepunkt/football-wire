## Group Narrative
The group of draws. Four matches planned, four draws delivered. Every
team on 1 point. Every team still alive. The most open group at the
tournament.

Belgium's struggle against Egypt was the headline — the golden
generation needed an own goal to rescue a point. De Bruyne and Lukaku
couldn't break down a disciplined Egyptian defence. If Belgium can't
create against Egypt, what happens against Iran's organized block?

Egypt have a right to feel robbed: led for 47 minutes, conceded only
an own goal. Salah and Marmoush's counter-attacking partnership
wasn't fully deployed. The best might be yet to come.

Iran's 2-2 with New Zealand was the most entertaining match.
Twice behind, twice back. Rezaeian and Mohebbi's equalizers showed
character. But being behind twice to the group's perceived weakest
team is a concern.

New Zealand's Elijah Just scored twice and announced himself as the
group's surprise package. A Motherwell player scoring a World Cup
brace. The romance is real. But two leads thrown away will haunt them.
