## Matchday 1 Results (June 12)
- USA 4-1 PAR — Bobadilla OG 7', Balogun 31' 45+5', Reyna 90+8' /
  Mauricio 73'. The co-hosts opened with a statement. Balogun's brace
  inside the first 45, Reyna's stoppage-time exclamation. Paraguay scored
  but were outclassed. A dominant display that the 4-1 scoreline accurately
  reflects.
- AUS 2-0 TUR — Irankunda 27', Metcalfe 75'. The teenager and the St.
  Pauli midfielder dismantled Turkey's tournament expectations in 90
  minutes. Güler, Yıldız, Çalhanoğlu — none of the stars delivered.

## Matchday 2 Results (June 19)
- USA 2-0 AUS — Burgess OG 11', Freeman 43'. USA controlled from the
  opening minutes. Another own goal from early pressure, then Freeman's
  goal confirmed the qualification. Australia fought but the quality gap
  was clear.
- TUR 0-1 PAR — Galarza 2'. Paraguay's 22-year-old Atlanta United
  midfielder scored in the second minute and the team defended with
  everything for 88 minutes. Turkey created chances but finished none.
  Zero goals in two matches. Crisis confirmed.

## Matchday 3 (June 24)

## Group Narrative
The co-host group delivered exactly what the USA needed and exactly what
Turkey feared. Pochettino's squad hit the ground running: 4-1, 2-0,
qualified before the final matchday. The Balogun-Reyna-Pulisic attacking
spine looked like a genuine tournament contender in both matches.

Turkey's collapse is the group's defining failure. Montella built a squad
around Güler (Real Madrid, born 2005) and Yıldız (Juventus, born 2005)
producing an attacking generation that was supposed to announce itself at
a World Cup. Zero goals from two matches. Çalhanoğlu's quality isolated.
The gap between expectation and delivery is unprecedented for a squad with
this profile.

Australia vs Paraguay on Matchday 3 is the tournament's first genuine
knockout-style group closer. Irankunda's emergence against Turkey was the
group stage's best individual story: 19 years old, South Sudanese family,
scored in his first World Cup match. The Socceroos have the momentum.
Paraguay have the desperation.

Almirón's presence adds emotional weight to Paraguay's campaign: the MLS
veteran wearing #10, Alfaro's trust in young Galarza paid off with the
winner vs Turkey. If they beat Australia, a 16-year World Cup absence
continues. If they lose, it's over again.
