# Group C

## Teams
- Brazil (BRA) — Five-time champions, Ancelotti's first WC, Neymar's last
- Morocco (MAR) — 2022 semi-finalists, Saibari's emergence, the defensive block
- Scotland (SCO) — First World Cup since 1998, McGinn's historic goal
- Haiti (HAI) — First World Cup since 1974, the miracle qualification story

Brazil and Morocco both qualified with 4 pts. MD3 determines group order.
Scotland on 3 pts are eliminated unless they beat Brazil and Morocco
lose to Haiti — an unlikely but mathematically possible combination.

## Matchday 1 Results
- BRA 1-1 MAR — Saibari 21' for Morocco. Vinicius Jr 32' for Brazil.
  The biggest shock of the group: Morocco led Brazil for 11 minutes
  and held on for a point against the favorites. Saibari's goal
  was composed; Vinicius' equalizer was inevitable but individual.
  Ancelotti's Brazil were not comfortable.
- HAI 0-1 SCO — McGinn 28'. Scotland's first World Cup goal in 28
  years. Haiti competed but couldn't level. A professional win for
  Clarke's side, historic in every way for Scotland.

## Matchday 2 Results
- SCO 0-1 MAR — Saibari 2'. The earliest goal of the group stage.
  Morocco led from the 2nd minute and defended it with discipline
  for 88 minutes. Scotland had chances but Morocco's block held.
  Saibari becomes the player of the group with a goal in each match.
- BRA 3-0 HAI — Cunha 23' 36', Vinicius Jr 45+3'. Brazil were
  professional if not spectacular. Cunha's brace in the first 36
  minutes settled it. Vinicius added a third before half time. Haiti
  had no answer for the quality gap.

## Matchday 3 (upcoming)
- SCO vs BRA — Mathematical elimination for Scotland unless they win
  and Morocco lose. More realistically: Scotland will compete hard
  for a creditable performance against the five-time champions in
  what may be the final match of their World Cup return.
- MAR vs HAI — Morocco to confirm second place. Haiti's chance to
  write a final chapter — their first-ever World Cup win would be one
  of the tournament's great stories. Ouahbi may rotate knowing the
  group position is secured.

## Group Narrative
Group C has been defined by its top two — Brazil and Morocco — and
by two stories of significant historical weight in Scotland and Haiti.

Morocco's performance against Brazil was the result of Matchday 1.
Not just a draw, but a lead maintained against the five-time champions.
Ismael Saibari, 25 years old, PSV midfielder, scored in the 21st
minute against Brazil and then again in the 2nd minute against
Scotland. In two matches across two matchdays he has been the
tournament's most consistently impactful individual performer in
the group. Morocco's defensive discipline — the same block that
took them to the 2022 semi-finals — remains intact under new coach
Ouahbi.

Brazil's 3-0 against Haiti was professional rather than commanding.
Matheus Cunha's brace in 13 minutes made it comfortable; Vinicius
added the third. But the 1-1 draw against Morocco raised the first
questions about Ancelotti's ability to translate his club-level
tactical mastery to the different rhythms of international football.
The squad has Neymar, Vinicius, Cunha, Raphinha, Endrick — and yet
the performances have been solid rather than dominant.

Scotland's return after 28 years has been handled well. McGinn's
goal against Haiti was the emotional peak of their tournament — 28
years of absence ended by their captain scoring in the 28th minute.
The loss to Morocco was realistic rather than disappointing: Saibari's
2nd-minute goal set a task that a well-organized Morocco was always
likely to defend. Clarke's team played with pride.

Haiti's tournament ends without a point or a goal — but that is not
the measure of their achievement. The story of qualification from a
nation dealing with ongoing crisis, the diaspora squad built across
France, USA, Canada, Belgium, and Switzerland, the presence of a
Premier League player (Bellegarde at Wolves) as their number 10 —
all of this matters more than the results. Migne deserves credit for
building something real from almost nothing.
