## Group Narrative
The host nation's group opened the tournament with drama and hasn't
stopped delivering. Mexico are through — Aguirre's pragmatic machine
conceding nothing, Jimenez's emotional return providing the story.
The Azteca at 2,240m is a fortress that other teams simply cannot
replicate.

The opening match set the tone for the entire World Cup: South
Africa's three red cards, Quinones scoring the tournament's first
goal in 9 minutes, Jimenez's header dedicated to his late father.
Drama from minute one.

Korea's comeback against Czech Republic was the group's tactical
highlight — Hong Myung-bo's team showing the resilience of their
2002 ancestors. Son's influence is gravitational even when he isn't
scoring. The loss to Mexico was narrow and competitive.

Czech Republic's narrative is the most painful: led in both matches,
won neither. Krejci opened the scoring against Korea, Sadilek opened
against South Africa. Both times, the lead evaporated in the final
quarter. The late collapse pattern is structural, not accidental.

South Africa's three reds overshadowed a competitive first half
against Mexico. The draw with Czech Republic despite missing three
suspended players showed character. Mokoena's penalty was the moment
of the match.
