# Czech Republic (CZE)

## Tournament History
2nd World Cup as Czech Republic (2006, 2026). Czechoslovakia appeared
8 times (1934-1990, runners-up 1934 and 1962). Twenty years between
World Cups — didn't qualify for 2010, 2014, 2018, 2022. Qualified
through UEFA play-offs under Koubek (beat Ireland and Denmark on
penalties). The 2004 Euro semi-final generation (Nedved, Rosicky,
Cech) is ancient history. This is a rebuilding team.

## Current Squad
Coach: Miroslav Koubek (CZE). Domestic appointment, steady hand.
Assistants: Jan Rezek, Jan Suchoparek, Jaroslav Plasil (CZE — former
Bordeaux legend, 103 caps, 367 games for Bordeaux, the experienced
voice in the coaching setup).

System: 4-2-3-1 / 4-4-2. Organized, physical, direct. Czech
football's identity: hard work, set pieces, tall forwards. Not
flashy but structured.

Key players: Patrik Schick (#10, FW) Bayer Leverkusen — Euro 2020
hero (the halfway line goal vs Scotland). Clinical finisher when fit.
Tomas Soucek (#22, MF) West Ham — the box-to-box engine who arrives
late in the area. Aerial threat from midfield. Ladislav Krejci (#7,
DF) Wolves — scored the opener vs KOR. Modern ball-playing defender.

Slavia Praha core: NINE players from Slavia — Zima, Holes, Chaloupek,
Jurasek, Provod, Sadilek, Chory, Doudera, and Stanek (GK). The
domestic club backbone is stronger than any other squad at the
tournament. Built on familiarity.

Young talents: Hugo Sochurek (#25, MF) Sparta Praha — born June
2008, just 18 years old. One of the youngest players at the World
Cup. Alexandr Sojka (#24, MF) Viktoria Plzen — 23.

Adam Hlozek (#9, FW) Hoffenheim — 23, the most talented young
Czech forward. Physical, good in the air, can play across the line.

GK: Matej Kovar (#1) PSV Eindhoven — 26, established at a top
Eredivisie club. The long-term solution.

## Group A Results
- KOR 2-1 CZE (Matchday 1) — Krejci 59' gave CZE the lead. Then
  collapsed: Hwang 67', Oh 80'. A lead thrown away in the final
  quarter. Painful.
- CZE 1-1 RSA (Matchday 2) — Sadilek 6' early goal, then held on
  until Mokoena's 83' penalty equalized. Two matches, two late
  collapses. A pattern emerging.

1 pt from 2 matches. Must beat Mexico on MD3 — at the Azteca.
Almost certainly eliminated.

## Storylines
- The late collapse problem: led in both matches, won neither. Krejci
  59' then conceded twice. Sadilek 6' then conceded late penalty.
  This team cannot close games. The psychological fragility is the
  defining narrative.
- Twenty years between World Cups: the 2006 team (Cech, Rosicky,
  Nedved was retired by then) is a distant memory. This squad has
  no World Cup experience.
- Slavia Praha dominance: 9 players from one club. The familiarity
  is a strength in defensive structure but potentially a ceiling
  on individual quality.
- Schick needs to deliver: the Euro 2020 star hasn't impacted the
  tournament yet. If Czech Republic are going to survive, he needs
  to find the form that produced that halfway line goal.
- MD3 at the Azteca: beating Mexico at 2,240m altitude with 80,000+
  hostile fans is arguably the hardest task in the group stage.
  History says this is over.

## Observed Patterns
- Lead in both matches, won neither. Cannot close games.
- Early goals (Krejci 59', Sadilek 6') show the team can compete
  when the game is open
- Late-game fragility: concede when protecting leads. Fitness,
  concentration, or tactical rigidity?
- Physical, direct play — aerial threats from Soucek, Chory,
  Hlozek at set pieces
- Slavia Praha structure provides defensive organization but limited
  creativity from open play
