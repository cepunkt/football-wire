# Germany (GER)

## Tournament History
4x World Cup winners (1954, 1974, 1990, 2014). 20th appearance.
Back-to-back group stage exits in 2018 and 2022 shattered the
"Turniermannschaft" (tournament team) identity. 2014 feels like
ancient history. This squad carries the weight of redemption.

## Current Squad
Coach: Julian Nagelsmann (GER). Youngest coach at a World Cup at 38.
Appointed 2023 after Euro 2024 quarter-final exit. Bold selector —
willing to drop established names, promote youth, experiment with
positions.
Assistants: Mads Buttgereit (GER), Benjamin Huebner (GER), Benjamin
Glueck (GER), Albertus Schreuder (NED).

System: 4-2-3-1, flexible. High press, technical quality throughout.
Nagelsmann rotates aggressively mid-game — the triple sub at 60' vs
CIV was premeditated, not reactive.

Key axis: Musiala (#10, Bayern) - Wirtz (#17, Liverpool) creative
partnership. Both 23. The most exciting creative pairing in world
football. Havertz (#7, Arsenal) as false nine.

Defensive experiment: Kimmich (#6, Bayern) moved to centre-back from
midfield. Partnered with Rudiger (#2, Real Madrid), Tah (#4, Bayern),
or Schlotterbeck (#15, Dortmund).

GK: Manuel Neuer (#1, Bayern) — 40 years old, still the starter.
8th tournament. Sweeper-keeper pioneer.

Depth: Undav (#26, Stuttgart) proved his worth as super-sub. Amiri
(#20, Mainz) and Leweling (#9, Stuttgart) also impacted vs CIV.
Ouedraogo (#25, Leipzig) born 2006, youngest in the squad.

Bayern contingent: Neuer, Kimmich, Pavlovic, Musiala, Goretzka, Tah
— 6 players from one club. Stuttgart also well represented: Leweling,
Stiller, Undav, Nubel.

## Storylines
- Redemption arc: after 2018 (last in group) and 2022 (out in group
  despite beating Spain), this is Germany's chance to prove the
  Turniermannschaft identity still exists. 6 pts from 2 games is the
  perfect start.
- The Nagelsmann rotation gamble: pulling Musiala, Sane, Pavlovic at
  60' against CIV when trailing 0-1 was either genius or insanity. It
  was genius — all three subs involved in the goals. But it reveals a
  coach who trusts his bench as much as his starters.
- Kimmich at centre-back: the most versatile player in world football
  moved from his natural midfield role. Tactical flexibility or
  necessity? Free up the midfield for Pavlovic/Goretzka/Stiller?
- Musiala-Wirtz axis: the generational creative pairing. Bayern and
  Liverpool's playmakers combining for Germany. When both are on the
  pitch, the technical quality is suffocating.
- Neuer at 40: eighth tournament, still starting. How long can he go?
- The 7-1 echo: Germany 7-1 in a World Cup again. Last time (2014 vs
  Brazil) became the most famous scoreline in WC history.

## Observed Patterns
- Strong starters, willing to rotate aggressively mid-game
- Set piece delivery through Kimmich and Wirtz
- Struggle to break down compact defences from open play (CIV exposed
  this — 68 minutes to equalize despite 59% possession)
- Goals come from close range (Undav 6m, Undav 14m) not distance
- Super-sub culture: Nagelsmann's bench is a weapon, not a safety net
- Hydration break at 71' vs CIV killed momentum after equalizer
