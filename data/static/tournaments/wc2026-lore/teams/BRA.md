# Brazil (BRA)

## Tournament History
23rd World Cup appearance — every single edition since 1930, the only
nation to achieve this. Five titles (1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, 2002)
— the most of any nation. The 2022 cycle ended in brutal fashion: quarter-final
penalty shootout loss to Croatia, Neymar's tears. The 2014 home
tournament produced the 7-1 humiliation against Germany. This squad
carries the weight of two devastating memories and the eternal
obligation to win every tournament they enter.

## Current Squad
Coach: Carlo Ancelotti (ITA). In his first World Cup as a manager —
after a career that includes Champions League titles at Milan,
Chelsea, Real Madrid, Bayern Munich, and Real Madrid again. The
most decorated club manager in history, now attempting to translate
that authority to international football. His son Davide is his
assistant, maintaining the family dynamic from Real Madrid.
Assistants: Davide Ancelotti (son, ITA), Francesco Mauri (ITA),
Juan (BRA), Paul Clement (ENG).

System: 4-2-3-1 / 4-3-3. Ancelotti's favored structure: defensive
solidity through a double pivot, creative freedom for the attackers.
Neymar behind the striker as the 10, Vinicius Jr on the left.

GK: Alisson (#1, Liverpool) — the best goalkeeper in the world at
his peak, born 1992-10-02. Ederson Moraes (#23, Fenerbahce) and
Weverton (#12, Gremio, born 1987) as backups.

The star axis: Vinicius Junior (#7, Real Madrid, born 2000-07-12)
— the Ballon d'Or contender, left wing, explosive. One goal and one
assist in two matches. Neymar (#10, Santos) — born 1992-02-05, the
34-year-old legend back for a final tournament. Returned to Santos to
regain match fitness. His last World Cup. Raphinha (#11, Barcelona)
— the right winger, industrious and direct. Matheus Cunha (#9,
Manchester United) — brace against Haiti (23', 36'), physical and
technical striker.

Midfield: Bruno Guimaraes (#8, Newcastle) — the engine room,
Newcastle's best player. Casemiro (#5, Manchester United) — the
destroyer, born 1992-02-23. Lucas Paqueta (#20, Flamengo) — the
creative link. Ederson Silva (#2, Atalanta) — utility midfielder.

Defence: Marquinhos (#4, PSG) — the captain, elite centre-back.
Gabriel Magalhaes (#3, Arsenal) — his defensive partner. Bremer
(#14, Juventus) — powerful centre-back option.

Young talent: Endrick (#19, Lyon, born 2006-07-21) — 19 years old,
prodigy signed by Real Madrid but loaned to Lyon. The future of
Brazilian football. Rayan (#26, Bournemouth, born 2006-08-03) —
also 19. Gabriel Martinelli (#22, Arsenal) brings pace and power.

Veterans: Alex Sandro (#6, Flamengo, born 1991-01-26), Danilo Luiz
(#13, Flamengo, born 1991-07-15) — senior defensive presence.
Fabinho (#17, Al Ittihad) — the Saudi Arabia-based midfielder.

## Storylines
- Ancelotti's first World Cup: 70 years old, five Champions League
  trophies, first time managing at a World Cup. The chess match is
  different at international level — he only meets these players
  periodically, can't drill specific patterns over months. The 1-1
  vs Morocco showed the adjustment required.
- Neymar's final tournament: at 34, back from injury via Santos,
  this is Neymar's last real shot at the only trophy that has eluded
  him. Brazil's most capped scorer with 79 goals. Every match, every
  touch, carries the weight of a generation's unfulfilled promise.
- Vinicius Jr as the talisman: after years of individual excellence
  at Real Madrid, this is Vinicius carrying Brazil rather than
  supplementing Neymar. A goal in each match. The new face of
  Selecao.
- Matheus Cunha's brace: the Manchester United striker scored twice
  in 13 minutes against Haiti in a performance that secured his
  starting position. The Cunha vs Neymar dynamic behind Vinicius is
  Brazil's key tactical question.
- Endrick and Rayan: two teenagers born in 2006 are in the squad.
  Endrick at 19 is the most hyped Brazilian prodigy since Ronaldo.
  Every substitute appearance carries enormous expectation.

## Observed Patterns
- Vinicius Jr's directness on the left is the primary threat —
  he has scored in both matches and draws double coverage every
  game
- Alisson's distribution and sweeping starts Brazil's build-up;
  his footwork is as important as his shot-stopping
- Slow to find rhythm against organized defences — Morocco held
  them at 0-0 for 31 minutes with a well-drilled low block
- Cunha's movement in the half-spaces is devastating against teams
  that overcommit to stopping Vinicius; the Haiti brace exploited
  exactly this
- Ancelotti rotates sparingly — the starting XI has been consistent,
  with bench impact reserved for managing leads rather than chasing games
