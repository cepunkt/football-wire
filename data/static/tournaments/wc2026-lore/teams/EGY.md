# Egypt (EGY)

## Tournament History
4th World Cup appearance (1934, 1990, 2018, 2026). Long absences
define Egypt's WC history — 56 years between 1934 and 1990, 28 years
between 1990 and 2018. The Salah generation brought them back.
2018: group stage exit. Now back for the second time in a row.

## Current Squad
Coach: Hossam Hassan (EGY). Egyptian legend — all-time top scorer
for Egypt with 69 international goals. Appointed 2024. The emotional
appointment — a national hero managing the national team.
Assistants: Mohamed Abdelwahed, Tarek Soliman.

Key players: Mohamed Salah (#10, FW) Liverpool — the Egyptian King.
34 years old, still scoring 20+ per season at Liverpool. The most
famous Egyptian sportsperson in history. Everything runs through him.
Omar Marmoush (#22, FW) Manchester City — the breakout star. Moved
to City from Frankfurt. Quick, direct, clinical. The present and
future alongside Salah.

Hamza Abdelkarim (#9, FW) Barcelona — born January 2008, just 18
years old. At BARCELONA. One of the youngest players in World Cup
history. The Egyptian prodigy.

Emam Ashour (#8, MF) Al Ahly — scored the opening goal against
Belgium. Midfield drive and energy.
Trezeguet (#7, FW) Al Ahly — experienced, knows the big stage.

Domestic backbone: 7 Al Ahly players, Al Ahly/Zamalek/Pyramids core.
GK El Shenawy (#1) at 37 is the veteran presence.

## Storylines
- Salah's World Cup: at 34, this is likely his last. 2018 was
  compromised by a shoulder injury from the Champions League final.
  This is the clean shot at a World Cup performance.
- Marmoush as the new generation: Man City's Egyptian forward is
  the heir apparent to Salah. Together they form a terrifying
  counter-attacking partnership.
- Abdelkarim at 18: Barcelona's teenager at a World Cup. If he
  plays significant minutes, he writes history.
- The Belgium result: Egypt outplayed Belgium for stretches. Led
  through Ashour's goal. Only denied by an own goal. This team
  believes it can compete with anyone.
- Hossam Hassan's emotional connection: the most-capped Egyptian
  ever managing the team. The nation's football identity personified.

## Observed Patterns
- Organized, compact defensive structure — hard to break down
- Counter-attacking through Salah and Marmoush's pace
- Midfield energy through Ashour
- Vulnerable to set pieces (own goal came from pressure situations)
- Domestic core provides defensive cohesion
