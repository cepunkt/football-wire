# Ecuador (ECU)

## Tournament History
5th World Cup appearance (2002, 2006, 2014, 2022, 2026). Best result:
Round of 16 (2006). Consistent CONMEBOL qualifiers in the modern era.
Finished 2nd in CONMEBOL qualifying with the best defensive record:
13 clean sheets, only 5 goals conceded in 18 matches. But just 14
scored — defense-first identity is structural, not circumstantial.

## Current Squad
Coach: Sebastian Beccacece (ARG, appointed August 2024). Bielsa
disciple. Obsessive video and data analyst. Defense-first 4-4-2 /
4-2-3-1 system with two banks of four and aggressive pressing triggers.
Quick diagonal counter-attacks on the break.
Assistants: Guillermo Marino (ARG), Nicolas Chiesa (ARG).

Key players: Moises Caicedo (#23, MF) — Chelsea's midfield anchor,
the engine. Willian Pacho (#6, DF) at PSG, Piero Hincapie (#3, DF)
at Arsenal — elite centre-back pairing. Pervis Estupinan (#7, DF) at
AC Milan, overlapping left-back. Enner Valencia (#13, FW) captain at
36, four World Cups now. Kendry Paez (#10, MF) aged just 19 — the
future, already the present.

Generation split: experienced spine (Valencia 36, Galindez 39) and
young talent (Paez 19, Ordoñez 22, Medina 21).

## Group E Results
- CIV 1-0 ECU (Matchday 1) — Lost to a 90th minute Amad Diallo goal.
  Dominated defensively but created little. Classic Beccacece game
  that narrowly went wrong.

## Storylines
- The Beccacece paradox: best defence in qualifying, but can they
  score? 14 goals in 18 CONMEBOL qualifiers. Organization without
  cutting edge.
- Enner Valencia's farewell tour — 4th World Cup at 36. Scored in
  3 previous World Cups (2014, 2022). Ecuador's all-time top scorer.
  Last dance.
- Kendry Paez at 19: signed to River Plate, already the creative
  fulcrum. Born 2007. Could be the face of Ecuadorian football for
  the next decade.
- The defensive spine is genuinely world class: Hincapie (Arsenal),
  Pacho (PSG), Caicedo (Chelsea). Three players worth combined
  ~250M EUR playing for a nation of 18M people.
- Must-win against CUW after Matchday 1 loss. A draw likely
  eliminates both teams. CIV-GER result determines the permutations.

## Observed Patterns
- Extremely compact defensive block, hard to break down
- Counter-attacking transitions through Paez and wide players
- Set pieces important — tall centre-backs (Torres, Hincapie, Pacho)
- Limited plan B when chasing a game — Beccacece's system is built
  to protect leads, not chase them
- GK situation: Galindez at 39 is experienced but aging
