# Norway (NOR)

## Tournament History
4th World Cup appearance (1938, 1994, 1998, 2026). Best: Round of 16 in
1998 (beat Brazil 2-1 in the group stage, lost to Italy in R16). 1994 was
a group stage exit. 28-year absence ends in 2026. The 1998 squad beat
Brazil. The 2026 squad has Haaland.

## Current Squad
Coach: Stale Solbakken (NOR). Long-time Norway manager, returned to the
role in 2020. Stabilising force. Won domestic titles with Rosenborg and
FK Copenhagen in his career. Trusted by the federation through years of
near-misses in qualifying.
Assistants: Kent Bergersen (NOR), Brede Hangeland (NOR — former Fulham and
Crystal Palace CB, 91 caps, born 1981, now coaching).

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Built around Haaland's movement and Ødegaard's
creation. Direct when in transition, patient when building. Hangeland's
defensive organisation visible in the defensive structure.

GK: Ørjan Nyland (#1, Sevilla) — born 1990, the veteran starter.
Sander Tangvik (#12, Hamburg SV) born 2002. Egil Selvik (#13, Watford).

Defence: Kristoffer Ajer (#3, Brentford) — the reliable CB and captain
candidate. Leo Østigård (#4, Genoa) born 1999 — scored vs Iraq 76'.
Marcus Holmgren Pedersen (#16, Torino). David Møller Wolfe (#5, Wolves)
born 2002. Torbjørn Heggem (#17, Bologna). Fredrik Bjørkan (#15,
Bodø/Glimt). Sondre Langås (#24, Derby) born 2001. Henrik Falchener (#25,

Midfield: Martin Ødegaard (#10, Arsenal) — the creative heart. When he

Attack: Erling Haaland (#9, Manchester City) — born 2000, 25 years old.

Bodø/Glimt contingent: Berg, Bjørkan, Hauge — three from the Norwegian

## Storylines
- Haaland's moment: 28 years since Norway last played a World Cup. In the
  first match back, their greatest ever striker scored twice in 43 minutes.
  The wait was worth it, at least so far.
- The 28-year gap: Norway qualified in 1994 and 1998 and vanished from the
  World Cup for 28 years. An entire generation of Norwegians grew up without
  their national team at this stage. Now Haaland and Ødegaard have ended
  the drought.
- Hangeland's transition: Brede Hangeland was one of the best defenders in
  Norwegian football history. Now he's coaching the defenders of the next
  generation as Solbakken's assistant. The knowledge transfer is direct.
- Sørloth vs Haaland: Norway have two world-class centre-forwards. Sørloth
  at Atletico Madrid, Haaland at Man City. Most nations would be delighted
  with one. Managing the rotation is Solbakken's luxury problem.
- Nusa at 20: Antonio Nusa, born in Belgium to Congolese parents, chose
  Norway. The RB Leipzig winger born 2005 represents Norway's diversity —
  and their European pipeline of talent outside the domestic league.

## Observed Patterns
- Haaland's movement: his first goal was a run in behind; his second was
  a penalty-area predator's finish — both goals demonstrated different
  aspects of his complete striker profile
- Ødegaard as conductor: his distribution unlocked Iraq's defensive line,
  especially for the first goal — Arsenal's captain in club form
- Østigård's box delivery: the centre-back's goal vs Iraq showed Norway's
  set piece threat from CB runs
- Berge's physicality: the Fulham midfielder won the midfield battle
  comprehensively — Norway's engine in the middle third
- Counter-attack efficiency: once Iraq equalised briefly, Norway didn't
  panic but systematically rebuilt dominance — tournament composure
