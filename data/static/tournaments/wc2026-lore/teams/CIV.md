# Ivory Coast (CIV)

## Tournament History
4th World Cup appearance (2006, 2010, 2014, 2026). Missed 2018 and
2022. Best result: Group stage. The Drogba generation never broke
through at a World Cup. This is a new chapter — AFCON 2023 champions
arriving with continental confidence.

## Current Squad
Coach: Emerse Fae (CIV). Former international midfielder. Took over
mid-AFCON 2023 when Jean-Louis Gasset was sacked, then won the whole
tournament on home soil. Kept the job. First World Cup as coach.
Assistants: Alain Gouamene (CIV), Guy Demel (CIV — 35 caps, 199
apps for Hamburg, 69 PL apps for West Ham).

System: Compact defensive structure, clinical counter-attacking.
Willing to sit deep and hit on transitions. Pragmatic, not pretty.

Key players: Amad Diallo (#15, FW) Manchester United — the star.
90th minute winner vs Ecuador. AFCON hero. 23 years old, massive
season at United. Franck Kessie (#8, MF) Al Ahli — experienced
midfield presence, ex-AC Milan. Seko Fofana (#6, MF) Porto — power
and drive in transition. Ibrahim Sangare (#18, MF) Nottingham Forest
— the destroyer. Odilon Kossounou (#7, DF) Atalanta — elite
centre-back.

Defence: Kossounou (Atalanta), Ndicka (#21, Roma), Diomande (#2,
Sporting CP), Singo (#5, Galatasaray) — genuine European quality
across the back line.

Young generation: Yan Diomande (#11, FW) RB Leipzig — born November
2006, just 19. Bazoumana Toure (#24, FW) Hoffenheim — born March
2006, 20. Christ Inao Oulai (#26, MF) Trabzonspor — born April 2006,
20. Three players born in 2006 in the squad.

GK: Yahia Fofana (#1) — outstanding vs Germany (MOTM candidate).
Saved everything outside the 6-yard box. Only 25, plays in Turkey.

## Group E Results
- CIV 1-0 ECU (Matchday 1) — Amad Diallo 90'. Classic smash-and-grab.
  Ecuador dominated possession but CIV defended deep and struck at
  the death. Diallo's run and finish was world class.
- GER 2-1 CIV (Matchday 2) — Kessie 30' put CIV ahead. Led until
  68' when Undav equalized. Lost to a 90'+4' winner. Fofana (GK)
  was extraordinary — kept CIV in it for 60 minutes. Triple sub at
  75' (Adingra, Seko Fofana, Guessand ON) nearly paid off when CIV
  had a free counter at 89' but the receiving player took an extra
  touch. Should have been 1-2 CIV.

## Storylines
- The AFCON champions are for real: beat Ecuador, pushed Germany to
  the final minute. This is not a team that makes up numbers.
- Amad Diallo's tournament: the Man United winger is a World Cup star
  in the making. 90th minute winners are how legends begin.
- Yahia Fofana's emergence: the GK was the best player on the pitch
  against Germany. If CIV go deep, he'll be a major reason why.
- The 89th minute counter vs Germany that got away: one touch too
  many cost CIV what would have been a famous win. Fine margins.
- Emerse Fae's pragmatism: this team doesn't need the ball. They
  defend compact, trust their athletes in transition, and strike
  when the moment comes. Anti-football to purists, devastating to
  opponents.

## Observed Patterns
- Defensive discipline: compact shape, hard to break down from open
  play. Germany needed super-sub magic to crack them.
- Counter-attacking threat: Diallo, Adingra, Guessand all have
  devastating pace on the break
- Clinical finishing: 2 goals from limited chances across 2 matches.
  No waste.
- Set piece vulnerability unclear — Germany's goals were open play
- GK Fofana is a genuine difference-maker
- Fatigue risk: CIV defended deep for long periods in both matches.
  Three games in 11 days is demanding for that style.
