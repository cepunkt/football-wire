# Senegal (SEN)

## Tournament History
4th World Cup appearance (2002, 2018, 2022, 2026). Best: QF 2002 (beat
France 1-0 in group opener, beat Sweden in R16, lost to Turkey on golden
goal). AFCON 2021 winners, AFCON 2025 winners under Thiaw. 2022 WC: R16,
lost to England 3-0. This squad is the post-Mane transition.

## Current Squad
Coach: Pape Thiaw (SEN). Born 1981, 45 years old. Appointed December
2024. Won AFCON 2025 (beat Morocco 1-0 in final) — youngest AFCON-
winning coach. First World Cup in charge.
Assistants: Aymen Djedidi (TUN), Teddy Pellerin (FRA).

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Attacking ambition tempered by Thiaw's youth
and inexperience at this level. Mane and Koulibaly as the experienced
spine; youth increasingly prominent.

GK: Yehvann Diouf (#1, OGC Nice) — born 1999, relatively young for a
starter. Édouard Mendy (#16, Al Ahli) — the Chelsea-era hero, now backup
at 33. Mory Diaw (#23, Le Havre) third choice.

Defence: Kalidou Koulibaly (#3, Al Hilal) — born 1991, the captain, the
wall. 34 years old and still commanding. This is his final World Cup.
Abdoulaye Seck (#4, Maccabi Haifa) born 1992 — veteran alongside him.
Moussa Niakhaté (#19, Lyon). Ismail Jakobs (#14, Galatasaray). Krépin
Diatta (#15, Monaco) — used as a wide option. Mamadou Sarr (#2, Chelsea)
born 2005. El Hadji Malick Diouf (#25, West Ham) born 2004. Antoine
Mendy (#24, OGC Nice) born 2004. Three defenders born 2004-2005 — real
youth investment.

Midfield: Idrissa Gueye (#5, Everton) — born 1989, the oldest outfielder,
the warrior. Lamine Camara (#8, Monaco) born 2004 — the most exciting
midfield talent. Pape Matar Sarr (#17, Tottenham) born 2002. Habib Diarra
(#21, Sunderland) born 2004. Pathé Ciss (#6, Rayo Vallecano).
Bara Sapoko Ndiaye (#22, Bayern Munich) born 2007 — the youngest player
in the entire squad.

Attack: Sadio Mané (#10, Al Nassr) — born 1992. Back at a World Cup at 34
after the Qatar heartbreak (he missed the tournament through injury in 2022).
Nicolas Jackson (#11, Bayern Munich) — the number nine from Chelsea-to-
Bayern's pipeline. Iliman Ndiaye (#13, Everton) born 2000.
Assane Diao (#7, Como) born 2005. Bamba Dieng (#9, Lorient).
Ibrahim Mbaye (#20, PSG) born 2008 — the youngest player at this World Cup.
Ismaïla Sarr (#18, Crystal Palace).

## Storylines
- The post-Mane generation: Mane is still here, but at 34, carried the
  injury scar from 2022's absence. The squad around him is younger —
  Lamine Camara, Assane Diao, Mamadou Sarr, Mbaye born 2008. The baton
  is passing live.
- Mbaye at 17: Ibrahim Mbaye, born January 2008, PSG academy. He is not
  just the youngest Senegalese player at a World Cup — he is the youngest
  player in the entire tournament. He scored the consolation vs France in
  stoppage time. The future is present.
- Koulibaly's last stand: the captain born 1991 is 34 years old, playing
  in Saudi Arabia, and leading a squad of teenagers and 20-somethings.
  His experience is the glue holding a young team together under pressure.
- Thiaw's test: the youngest head coach in the African contingent is now
  managing a must-win situation in the second group game. No experience
  at this level. Complete sink-or-swim.
- Gueye at 36: Idrissa Gueye, born 1989, Everton midfielder, is the
  engine of a midfield that includes players 18 years younger. The old
  warrior and the teenagers.

## Observed Patterns
- Vulnerable to clinical opponents who wait for the second half: France
  absorbed their early pressure and struck after 66 minutes
- Young midfield exciting but inconsistent: Lamine Camara and Pape Matar
  Sarr have quality but are still developing tournament temperament
- Depth in attack: Mane, Jackson, I. Ndiaye, Diao, Sarr, Mbaye — the
  forward options suggest rotation and unpredictability
- Koulibaly's leadership vocal and physical: sets the defensive line,
  wins aerial duels, commands the younger defenders around him
- Gueye the insurance policy: his defensive midfield work covers for
  the more adventurous players ahead of him — the oldest outfielder doing
  the most unglamorous work
