# Austria (AUT)

## Tournament History
8th World Cup appearance. Best: 3rd place (1954). Long absence — last
appeared in 2018 qualifiers but didn't make it. Missed 2022. Back for
the first time since 1998. The Rangnick revolution transformed
Austrian football from also-rans to genuine competitors. Euro 2024
Round of 16 was the foundation. Now they're at a World Cup.

## Current Squad
Coach: Ralf Rangnick (GER). The godfather of Gegenpressing. Former
RB Leipzig, Manchester United interim. Turned down Bayern Munich to
stay with Austria. His tactical identity IS the team: high press,
aggressive transitions, positional play. The most tactically
distinctive coach in the tournament alongside Bielsa.
Assistants: Lars Kornetka (GER), Stefan Oesen, Onur Cinel.

System: 4-2-3-1 / 4-4-2 press. Rangnick's trademark: aggressive
high press, vertical transitions, intense running. The Salzburg/
Leipzig pipeline provides the physical and tactical base. 23 of 26
players play abroad.

Key players: David Alaba (#8, DF) Real Madrid — the captain. World
class at CB or LB. Champions League winner, La Liga winner. Austria's
most decorated player ever. Career record: 2-0 vs Messi.
Marcel Sabitzer (#9, MF) Borussia Dortmund — the experienced
playmaker, set piece specialist. Konrad Laimer (#20, MF) Bayern
Munich — the pressing machine, Rangnick's archetype player.
Kevin Danso (#3, DF) Tottenham — aggressive, physical CB.

Romano Schmid (#18, MF) Werder Bremen — scored Austria's first
World Cup goal in 28 YEARS (21' vs Jordan). Historic.

Marko Arnautovic (#7, FW) Red Star Belgrade — 37 years old, the
veteran target man. Scored the penalty at 90'+12' vs Jordan. The
elder statesman who's been through everything.

Bundesliga dominance: 14 of 26 players in the German league.
Rangnick literally built a Bundesliga national team.

Interesting: Carney Chukwuemeka (#17, MF) Dortmund — born in England,
chose Austria. Paul Wanner (#24, MF) PSV — born December 2005, 20.
Dual German-Austrian nationality, chose Austria.

## Group J Results
- AUT 3-1 JOR (Matchday 1) — Schmid 21' (first Austrian WC goal in
  28 years!), Olwan 50' equalized for Jordan, then Al-Arab OG 76'
  and Arnautovic 90'+12' penalty. Austria needed 12 MINUTES of added
  time to seal it. Wild game. Cordoba 1978 energy in the stands.

3 pts. Job done, but not convincing.

## Storylines
- Schmid's historic goal: first Austrian World Cup goal since 1998.
  28 years of silence broken. The whole nation exhaled.
- Rangnick's project: turned down Bayern Munich for this. The
  tactical identity is clear — Austria press like their lives depend
  on it. When it works, it's devastating. When opponents absorb it,
  they're vulnerable.
- Alaba vs Messi: first ever competitive meeting between Austria and
  Argentina. Alaba has faced Messi in La Liga and Champions League
  through Real Madrid vs Barcelona. Monday is personal — the captain
  defending against the GOAT.
- Arnautovic at 37: the old warrior, still scoring, still celebrating,
  still Viennese. 90'+12' penalty to seal it. That's Arnautovic.
- The Bundesliga pipeline: Rangnick's network means Austrian players
  flow through German football. The tactical language is shared. The
  system is coherent because the players already speak Rangnick.
- Cordoba 1978: Austria 3-2 West Germany. Krankl's 88' winner. Edi
  Finger's radio call — "I WERDE NARRISCH!" — became a cultural
  monument. First Austrian win over Germany in 47 years. The Cordoba
  anniversary is today, June 21. Argentina is tomorrow. 48 years.
  Longest day of the year. The echoes write themselves.

## Observed Patterns
- High press creates turnovers in dangerous areas
- Set piece threat through Sabitzer's delivery and tall defenders
- Struggled to break down Jordan's defensive block for long periods
- 12 minutes of added time suggests game management issues
- Physical intensity but sustainability over 90' is a question
- Transition speed is the primary attacking weapon
