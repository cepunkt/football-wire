# Japan (JPN)

## Tournament History
8th consecutive World Cup appearance (1998-2026). Best result: Round
of 16 (2002, 2010, 2018, 2022 — four times). Giant-killers in 2022
— beat Germany and Spain in the group stage before losing to Croatia
on penalties. Consistent overachievers with a deep European talent
pool.

## Current Squad
Coach: Hajime Moriyasu (JPN). In charge since 2018. Longest-serving
Japan coach. Survived the heartbreak of 2022 penalty exit vs Croatia.
System: 3-4-2-1 / 4-2-3-1 flexible. Organized central press,
drop-forward build-up creating 3-2-1 overloads, disciplined transitions.
Assistants: Toshihide Saito, Hiroshi Nanami, Ryoichi Maeda.

Heavily European squad — 23 of 26 players based in Europe. Bundesliga
pipeline dominant (6 players).

Key players: Takefusa Kubo (#8, MF) Real Sociedad — the creative
spark. Daichi Kamada (#15, MF) Crystal Palace — rescued the NED draw
with 88' equalizer. Ao Tanaka (#7, MF) Leeds — engine of the midfield.
Wataru Endo (#6, MF) Monchengladbach — the anchor. Hiroki Ito (#21,
DF) Bayern Munich — elite centre-back.

Age profile: Yuto Nagatomo (#5) at 39 is the elder statesman — first
Japanese player at five World Cups (2010-2026). Former Inter Milan
(200+ apps). Goto (#9, 21) and Shiogai (#26, 21) represent the next
generation.

## Group F Results
- NED 2-2 JPN (Matchday 1) — Van Dijk 51', Summerville 64' for NED.
  Nakamura 57', Kamada 88' for JPN. Japan came from behind twice.
  Showed resilience and quality against a side that later put 5 past
  Sweden.

## Storylines
- 2022 echo: Japan beat Germany and Spain in Qatar before falling in
  the knockouts. Can this generation go further? The squad is more
  European-integrated than ever.
- The Kamada moment: 88th minute equalizer against the Netherlands.
  Crystal Palace's playmaker showing his best on the biggest stage.
- Kubo's tournament: Real Sociedad's star needs a World Cup performance
  to match his club form. 24 years old, second WC after 2022.
- Moriyasu's tactical flexibility: switched between 3-back and 4-back
  against Netherlands depending on phase of play. The defensive
  organization is genuinely world class.
- European integration: this might be the most technically gifted
  Japan squad ever. Ajax, Bayern, Leeds, Crystal Palace, Freiburg,
  Bremen — depth across top leagues.

## Observed Patterns
- Comeback resilience: twice behind vs NED, twice equalized
- Second half improvement — both goals came after half-time (57', 88')
- Transition speed: quick vertical play through Kubo and Kamada
- Defensive discipline: organized in a low/mid block, hard to break
- Set piece vulnerability: Van Dijk headed NED's opener from a corner
