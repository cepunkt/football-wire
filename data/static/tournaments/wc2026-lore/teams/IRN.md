# Iran (IRN)

## Tournament History
7th World Cup appearance (1978, 1998, 2006, 2014, 2018, 2022, 2026).
Consistent Asian qualifiers. Best: Group stage, but competitive —
drew with Portugal 1-1 in 2018, nearly beat them. Beat Wales 2-0 in
2022. Three WC wins total (USA 1998, Morocco 2018, Wales 2022).
Always organized, always difficult to beat, rarely spectacular.

## Current Squad
Coach: Amir Ghalenoei (IRN). Domestic appointment. Second spell in
charge (2006-07, then 2023-present). Led Iran to 2023 Asian Cup
semi-finals — best since 1976. Conservative, defensive structure.
Assistants: Saeed Alhoei, Andranik Timotian, Human Afazeli,
Rahman Rezaei (5 assistants — large staff).

Key players: Mehdi Taremi (#9, FW) Olympiacos — the clinical
striker. Former Porto, Inter Milan. Iran's most dangerous attacking
player. Alireza Beiranvand (#1, GK) Tractor — the penalty specialist
from 2018 (saved Ronaldo's penalty). Experienced, commanding.
Alireza Jahanbakhsh (#7, MF) — the experienced creative presence.

Heavily domestic squad: 15 of 26 players in the Iranian league.
Persepolis, Esteghlal, Tractor, Sepahan, Foolad — the domestic clubs
provide structure.

Interesting selections: Dennis Eckert (#24, FW) Standard Liege —
German-Iranian, adds European quality. Razzaghinia (#26, MF)
Esteghlal — born April 2006, just 20. Youngest in the squad.

Veteran spine: Hajsafi (#3, 36), Khalilzadeh (#4, 37), Rezaeian
(#23, 36) — experienced defenders who've been through multiple
World Cup campaigns.

## Group G Results

  Rezaeian 32', Mohebbi 64' for Iran. Twice behind, twice equalized.
  A draw against New Zealand should have been a win. Dropped points

1 pt. Mixed feelings.

## Storylines
- The experienced core: Iran's spine (Hajsafi, Khalilzadeh, Rezaeian,
  Taremi, Beiranvand) have been through this before. They know World
  Cup football. Experience versus youth is Iran's identity.
- Twice behind, twice back: resilience against New Zealand showed
  character. But being 0-1 after 7 minutes and 1-2 after 54 minutes
  against the weakest team in the group is concerning.
- Taremi needs to deliver: the biggest Iranian talent of this
  generation. At 34, this is his last World Cup. Needs to be the
  difference maker.
- Iran's eternal World Cup identity: organized, difficult, never
  quite breaking through. Can this group — the most open they'll ever
  face — finally produce a Round of 16 appearance?

## Observed Patterns
- Slow starters — conceded at 7' and were behind at 54'
- Comeback ability — twice equalized, never gave up
- Defensive organization but vulnerable to pace and directness
- Counter-attacking through Taremi and Ghayedi
- Set piece threat through experienced defenders
