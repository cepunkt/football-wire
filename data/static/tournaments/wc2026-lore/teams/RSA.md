# South Africa (RSA)

## Tournament History
4th World Cup appearance (1998, 2002, 2010, 2026). Hosts in 2010 —
the vuvuzela World Cup. Failed to qualify for 2014, 2018, 2022.
Return after 16 years is significant. Best result: Group stage
(have never advanced to the knockouts). Bafana Bafana carry the
weight of being Africa's most famous underperforming football nation.

## Current Squad
Coach: Hugo Broos (BEL). Appointed 2021. Belgian disciplinarian who
rebuilt the team with young domestic talent. Won AFCON 2017 with
Cameroon. Knows African football intimately.
Assistant: Helman Mkhalele (RSA).

System: Compact defensive 4-3-3. Built around Mamelodi Sundowns'
core — domestic cohesion over individual European quality. Counter-
attacking transitions through the pace of Appollis and Mofokeng.

Key players: Ronwen Williams (#1, GK) Mamelodi Sundowns — the star.
CAF Goalkeeper of the Year multiple times. World class shot-stopper,
commands the area. The most important player in the squad.
Teboho Mokoena (#4, MF) Sundowns — scored the penalty vs CZE (83').
Long-range shooting threat, set piece delivery.
Lyle Foster (#9, FW) Burnley — the only Premier League player.
Physical, holds the ball up, links play. Relebohile Mofokeng (#10,
FW) Orlando Pirates — born October 2004, 21. Rapid, direct, the
future of South African football.

Domestic backbone: 8 Sundowns players, 7 Orlando Pirates. The squad
is built on South Africa's two biggest clubs.

Notable: Mbekezeli Mbokazi (#14, DF) at Chicago Fire, Olwethu
Makhanya (#24, DF) at Philadelphia Union — MLS contingent.

## Group A Results
- MEX 2-0 RSA (Matchday 1) — Opening match. Competitive first half
  but three red cards (Sithole 49', Zwane 82', Montes 90'+2')
  destroyed the game plan. Finished with 9 men. A record for a WC
  opening match.
- CZE 1-1 RSA (Matchday 2) — Sadilek 6' for CZE, Mokoena 83'
  penalty for RSA. Battled back from behind despite missing three
  suspended players from the red cards. Showed resilience and
  character.

1 pt from 2 matches. Need a result vs KOR on MD3.

## Storylines
- The red card catastrophe: three reds in the opening match
  overshadowed what was a competitive first 49 minutes against Mexico.
  The suspensions cascaded into MD2, yet they still salvaged a draw
  against Czech Republic. Character.
- Mokoena's penalty: 83rd minute equalizer against CZE. Showed nerve
  under pressure. The kind of moment that builds tournament belief.
- Ronwen Williams as the foundation: without his goalkeeping, both
  results could have been worse. The CAF Goalkeeper of the Year is
  South Africa's most important player.
- Domestic-first squad: Broos chose cohesion over individual stars.
  The Sundowns/Pirates axis gives the team structure and familiarity
  that compensates for the quality gap against European squads.
- Mofokeng as the X-factor: young, fast, unpredictable. The kind of
  player who can change a game in an instant.
- MD3 vs South Korea is must-win. Need to better CZE's result to
  have any chance of progression.

## Observed Patterns
- Disciplinary vulnerability: 3 reds in one match is catastrophic
  at any level. Composure under pressure needs improvement.
- Resilient when behind: came from behind to draw 1-1 vs CZE
  despite missing 3 suspended players
- Counter-attacking pace through Appollis and Mofokeng
- Set piece threat: Mokoena's penalty + delivery from dead balls
- GK dependency: Williams is the difference between competitive
  results and heavy defeats
