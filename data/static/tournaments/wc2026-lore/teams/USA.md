# United States (USA)

## Tournament History
12th World Cup appearance. Co-hosts in 2026 alongside Canada and Mexico.
Best: 3rd place (1930). Modern era: R16 in 1994, 2010, 2014, 2022 (lost
to Netherlands 3-1). Missed only 2018. This is the generation that was
supposed to arrive — and they've arrived.

## Current Squad
Coach: Mauricio Pochettino (ARG). Appointed September 10, 2024.
Won nothing in club football yet built Chelsea's identity and took Tottenham
to a Champions League final. First assignment at a World Cup.
Assistants: Miguel Dagostino (ARG), Jesus Perez (ESP).

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Pressing intensity high, wing width through
Robinson and Dest. Midfield anchored by Adams, attacking creativity through
Reyna and Pulisic. Balogun as the number nine.

GK: Matt Turner (#1, New England Revolution) — returns to MLS as veteran
starter. Chris Brady (#25, Chicago Fire) born 2004, backup.

Defence: Antonee Robinson (#5, Fulham) — best left back in England's top
half. Sergiño Dest (#2, PSV) right. Chris Richards (#3, Crystal Palace)
and Joe Scally (#23, Mönchengladbach) as centre-back options.

Tim Ream (#13, Charlotte FC) — born 1987, the old guard. Captain material.
Alex Freeman (#16, Villarreal) — born 2004, right-back scored vs Australia.

Midfield: Tyler Adams (#4, Bournemouth) — the engine, press-trigger.
Weston McKennie (#8, Juventus) — box-to-box power.

Attack: Christian Pulisic (#10, AC Milan) — the captain, the best player.
Giovanni Reyna (#7, Mönchengladbach) — born 2002, Claudio's son, scored
90+8' vs Paraguay after fighting for fitness his whole career. Folarin
Balogun (#20, Monaco) — born 2001, brace in MD1. Timothy Weah (#21,
Marseille) — son of George.

## Group D Results
- USA 4-1 PAR (Matchday 1) — Bobadilla OG 7', Balogun 31' 45+5', Reyna
  90+8'. Mauricio 73' consolation for Paraguay. A statement from the hosts.
  Balogun brace in 45 minutes of first half; Reyna's injury-time goal was
  the exclamation point.
- USA 2-0 AUS (Matchday 2) — Burgess OG 11', Freeman 43'. Another
  controlled performance. Co-host qualified with 6 pts from 2 games.

USA QUALIFIED — 6 pts, GF 6, GA 1, GD +5.

## Storylines
- The co-host pressure: the USA haven't just shown up — they've dominated.
  4-1 and 2-0 in their first two games. Pochettino has delivered the start
  that 300 million Americans demanded.
- Balogun's transformation: chose the US over England, fought for selection,
  scored twice in his first World Cup match. Born in London, raised for
  Arsenal, now the face of American football's ambition.
- Reyna's redemption: Giovanni Reyna's career has been one of promise
  interrupted by injury. 90+8' against Paraguay was the story of a player
  who refused to be remembered as what might have been.
- Pulisic as captain and talisman: no goals yet, but Pochettino has built
  the system around the AC Milan captain. The two goals vs Australia came
  from the positions he creates.
- Tim Ream at 38, Freeman at 21: the oldest and one of the youngest in the
  squad both featured in the opening matches. The transition is happening
  in real time.

## Observed Patterns
- Clinical in front of goal: 6 goals from 2 matches, both opponents
  contributed own goals from relentless pressure
- Press-trigger: Adams sets the press, midfield follows. Paraguay and
  Australia both struggled to build out
- Wing depth creates dilemma for opponents: Weah, Aaronson, Zendejas, Wright
  rotate without quality drop
- Set piece threat: the OGs vs Paraguay (7') and Australia (11') both came
  from dangerous deliveries in early minutes
- Freeman emergence: the 21-year-old Villarreal full-back scored in MD2 —
  a genuine option at right back pushing Dest
