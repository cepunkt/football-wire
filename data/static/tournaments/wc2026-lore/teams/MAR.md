# Morocco (MAR)

## Tournament History
7th World Cup appearance (1970, 1986, 1994, 1998, 2018, 2022, 2026). The
2022 tournament in Qatar was the defining moment in Moroccan football
history: first African/Arab nation to reach the semi-finals. Eliminating
Spain, Portugal, and Belgium along the way. Lost to France in the
semi and Croatia in the third-place match, but the story was made.
Now the question is whether a new coach can build on that foundation
or whether 2022 was a once-in-a-generation peak.

## Current Squad
Coach: Mohamed Ouahbi (MAR). New appointment after Walid Regragui's
post-2022 era. Ouahbi inherits the core of the 2022 squad and is
tasked with replicating their defensive organization while finding
new attacking solutions. First major tournament in charge.
Assistants: Youssouf Hadji (MAR — ex-Nancy, Leicester), Joao
Sacramento (POR — former Villarreal and Sporting CP coach).

System: 4-3-3 / 5-4-1 in defense. The famous Morocco block —
disciplined, compact, hard to break. Hakimi marauding from right
back. High defensive line managed by elite centre-backs.

GK: Yassine Bounou (#1, Al Hilal) — the 2022 WC hero who saved
two penalties in the Spain shootout. Now at Al Hilal after leaving
Sevilla. Born 1991-04-05. Commanding presence.

The spine: Achraf Hakimi (#2, PSG) — the right wing-back who
generates from behind. Elite fullback. Sofyan Amrabat (#4, Real
Betis) — the defensive anchor, the warrior of 2022. Azzedine Ounahi
(#8, Girona) — the midfield engine who emerged at 2022. Brahim
Diaz (#10, Real Madrid) — the playmaker, dual nationality (Spain).
Nayef Aguerd (#5, Al Fateh) — senior centre-back. Noussair Mazraoui
(#3, Manchester United) — the versatile fullback.

Star of the group stage: Ismael Saibari (#11, PSV Eindhoven, born
2001-01-28) — 25 years old, scored in both matches. The breakthrough
performer. His goal vs Brazil in the 21st minute and his 2nd-minute
finish vs Scotland have been the defining moments of Morocco's
campaign.

Young talent: Ayyoub Bouaddi (#6, Lille, born 2007-10-02) — just
18 years old, youngest in the squad. Chemsdine Talbi (#7, Sunderland,
born 2005-05-09), Samir El Mourabet (#15, Strasbourg, born 2005-10-06),
Gessime Yassine (#16, Strasbourg, born 2005-11-22), Chadi Riad (#18,
Crystal Palace, born 2003-06-17), Bilal El Khannouss (#23, VfB
Stuttgart, born 2004-05-10), Ayoube Amaimouni (#21, Eintracht
Frankfurt, born 2004-11-30).

Forwards: Soufiane Rahimi (#9, Al Ain) — the striker. Ayoub El
Kaabi (#20, Olympiacos) — the Copa del Rey top scorer. Abde
Ezzalzouli (#17, Angers) — pacy winger. Neil El Aynaoui (#24,
Roma) — midfield depth.

## Storylines
- Saibari the revelation: scored in the 21st minute against Brazil
  and the 2nd minute against Scotland. Two goals in two matches, both
  first-half openers, both setting the tone for their result.
  The PSV midfielder has announced himself to the world.
- The 2022 legacy: Bounou, Hakimi, Amrabat, Mazraoui — the core
  of the 2022 semi-final squad is still here. The question is whether
  Ouahbi can replicate Regragui's tactical masterclass. Two matches
  in, the answer looks like yes.
- Holding Brazil to a draw: this was the group stage result of
  matchday 1. Morocco absorbed, defended, and punished on the break.
  It wasn't a fluke — it was the 2022 blueprint executed with the
  same personnel against a different opponent.
- The youth pipeline: Bouaddi (18), Talbi (20), El Mourabet (20),
  Yassine (20), El Khannouss (21) — Morocco have invested heavily in
  the next generation and multiple teenagers are in the actual squad.
  The 2030 World Cup will be co-hosted by Morocco. This squad is
  a transition piece.
- Ouahbi's first tournament: inheriting a team that reached a
  semi-final and maintaining their identity is harder than it sounds.
  Four points from two matches against Brazil and Scotland is a
  superb start.

## Observed Patterns
- The defensive block is the foundation: same Morocco structure as
  2022 — compact lines, deny space, win it back, transition quickly
- Saibari's runs from deep are the primary attacking weapon — he
  arrives late into spaces the defensive-minded structure creates
- Hakimi as the right wing-back gets forward constantly; his overlaps
  are the key source of width and crosses
- Amrabat as the defensive anchor is irreplaceable; he disrupts and
  recycles with a physicality few midfielders can match at this level
- Clinical finishing from limited chances: 2 shots, 2 goals in the
  first half across two matches. Ruthless conversion rate
