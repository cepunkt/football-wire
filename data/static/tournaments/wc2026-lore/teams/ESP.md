# Spain (ESP)

## Tournament History
17th World Cup appearance. Winners in 2010 (the tiki-taka peak).
Euro 2024 champions — the youngest squad to win a major tournament.
2022: lost to Morocco on penalties in the Round of 16. The post-2010
generation has underperformed at World Cups. But this team is
different: younger, faster, more direct than any Spanish side before.

## Current Squad
Coach: Luis de la Fuente (ESP). Won Euro 2024. Promoted through the
youth ranks. Quiet, methodical, trusts young players.
Assistants: Juanjo Gonzalez, Alberto de la Fuente (his son).

System: 4-3-3. Not the tiki-taka of old — this Spain is direct,
vertical, and explosive on the wings. Yamal and Nico Williams
changed the identity.

Key players: Lamine Yamal (#19, FW) Barcelona — born July 2007,
just 18 years old. Already a Euro champion. The most exciting
teenager in world football. Generational. Rodri (#16, MF) Man City —
the Ballon d'Or holder, the midfield metronome. When Rodri plays,
Spain's structure is impregnable. Pedri (#20, MF) Barcelona —
the interior genius. Gavi (#9, MF) Barcelona — the terrier.
Nico Williams (#17, FW) Athletic Club — explosive left winger,
the other half of the wing partnership.

Pau Cubarsi (#22, DF) Barcelona — born January 2007, 19. Centre-back
already starting for Barcelona and Spain. Generational defensive
talent.

Barcelona core: Yamal, Pedri, Gavi, Eric Garcia, Cubarsi, Dani Olmo,
Ferran Torres, Joan Garcia — 8 Barcelona players. The spine.

Arsenal contingent: Raya (GK), Merino, Zubimendi — 3 Gunners.

## Group H Results
- ESP 0-0 CPV (Matchday 1) — THE SHOCK. Spain couldn't score against
  Cape Verde. 60%+ possession, controlled the game, couldn't break
  through. Vozinha (GK, 39 years old) was heroic for Cape Verde.
  The biggest upset by the numbers in the group stage.

1 pt. Pressure building.

## Storylines
- Euro 2024 champions held by Cape Verde: the narrative writes itself.
  Spain are supposed to be among the favourites. Drawing 0-0 with the
  smallest team in the group creates immediate crisis.
- Yamal at 18: the youngest Euro champion, now at his first World Cup.
  The weight of expectation on a teenager is immense. If Spain go
  deep, he'll be the story. If they stumble, he'll carry none of the
  blame — but all of the spotlight.
- Cubarsi and Yamal — both born 2007: two teenagers defining Spain's
  future. The youngest spine in World Cup history.
- Rodri's importance: when he plays, Spain's structure works. The
  Ballon d'Or winner is the difference between Euro champions and
  the team that drew 0-0 with Cape Verde.
- De la Fuente under pressure: Euro 2024 bought credit, but the
  Cape Verde draw spends it fast. Spain need a reaction.

## Observed Patterns
- Possession dominance without penetration (0-0 vs CPV)
- Wing play through Yamal and Williams is the primary threat
- Vulnerable to deep-block defensive teams that deny space
- Midfield control through Rodri-Pedri axis
- Need to convert chances — creating isn't the problem, finishing is
