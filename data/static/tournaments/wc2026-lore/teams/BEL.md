# Belgium (BEL)

## Tournament History
15th World Cup appearance. Best: 3rd place 2018 (the "Golden
Generation" peak). 2022: group stage exit — the beginning of the end
for Hazard, Courtois-era Belgium. The golden generation is fading but
not gone: De Bruyne (#7, 35) and Lukaku (#9, 33) are still here.
Courtois (#1) still at Real Madrid.

## Current Squad
Coach: Rudi Garcia (FRA). The outsider choice — French coach for
Belgium. Appointed 2024 after Tedesco's departure. Pragmatic,
experienced (Lyon, Roma, Marseille, Napoli). Less romantic than
Martinez, more structured.
Assistants: Stephane Jobard (FRA), Claude Fichaux (FRA).

Key players: Kevin De Bruyne (#7, MF) Napoli — 35 years old, still
world class on his day. Moved from Man City to Napoli. Likely his
last World Cup. Romelu Lukaku (#9, FW) Napoli — Belgium's all-time
top scorer. De Bruyne and Lukaku reunited at Napoli.
Thibaut Courtois (#1, GK) Real Madrid — when fit, one of the best
GKs in the world. Jeremy Doku (#11, FW) Man City — the explosive
dribbler, the future. Amadou Onana (#24, MF) Aston Villa — midfield
power. Tielemans (#8, MF) Aston Villa — the creative passer.

Emerging: De Ketelaere (#17, FW) Atalanta — flourishing in Italy.
Debast (#2, DF) Sporting — 22, the new defensive leader.

## Storylines
- The golden generation's last dance: De Bruyne (35), Lukaku (33),
  Courtois (34), Witsel (37). This is it. 2018's 3rd place feels
  like a lifetime ago.
- The own goal equalizer: Belgium didn't actually score against Egypt.
  Mohamed Hany's own goal at 66' rescued a point. Is this a team
  that can genuinely create, or one surviving on reputation?
- Rudi Garcia as the anti-romance choice: no Belgian identity, pure
  pragmatism. The fans are skeptical. Results will determine legacy.
- De Bruyne-Lukaku at Napoli: club partnership carrying into the
  national team. When they connect, it's devastating. When they
  don't, Belgium look ordinary.

## Observed Patterns
- Struggled to create against organized defences (Egypt)
- Relying on individual quality rather than system
- Defensive solidity with Courtois but offensive patterns unclear
- Set piece threat through De Bruyne's delivery
