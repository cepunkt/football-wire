# Paraguay (PAR)

## Tournament History
9th World Cup appearance (1930, 1950, 1958, 1986, 1998, 2002, 2006, 2010,
2026). Best result: quarter-finals 2010 (beat Japan on penalties, lost to
Spain 1-0). Missed 2014, 2018, 2022. The 16-year absence ended through a
CONMEBOL qualifying campaign that went to the wire.

## Current Squad
Coach: Gustavo Alfaro (ARG). The Argentine nomad — managed Boca Juniors,
Atletico Tucuman, Ecuador (took them to 2022 World Cup group stage), and
now Paraguay. Experienced operator in CONMEBOL's brutal qualifying landscape.
Assistants: Claudio Cristofanelli, Carlos Gonzalez, Alejandro Manograsso.

System: Defensive solidity first, transition second. Alfaro's identity is
pragmatic — control the midfield, deny space, exploit set pieces and
moments of quality. Not a possession team.

GK: Gatito Fernández (#1, Cerro Porteño) — born 1988, the veteran anchor.
Gastón Olveira (#22, Olimpia) backup. Both domestic-based.

Defence: Fabián Balbuena (#5, Grêmio) born 1991, experienced CB. Gustavo
Gómez (#15, Palmeiras) captain, another 30+ veteran. Júnior Alonso (#6,
Atlético Mineiro). Omar Alderete (#3, Sunderland). Old guard, robust.
Alexandro Maidana (#26, Talleres) born 2005 — the youth exception.

Midfield: Andrés Cubas (#14, Vancouver Whitecaps). Diego Gómez (#8,
Brighton) born 2003 — the Premier League link. Ramón Sosa (#7, Palmeiras).
Matías Galarza (#23, Atlanta United) born 2002 — scored vs Turkey at 2'.

Attack: Miguel Almirón (#10, Atlanta United) — back in MLS, the thread
connecting old generation to new. Julio Enciso (#19, Strasbourg) born
2004 — the explosive winger. Antonio Sanabria (#9, Cremonese). Mauricio
(#11, Palmeiras) born 2001 — scored vs USA.

## Storylines
- Alfaro's South American duality: he took Ecuador to the 2022 World Cup
  quarter-final, now he's leading Paraguay against them in the same
  tournament. The Argentine coach is the continent's most adaptable tactician.
- Galarza's 2-minute winner: Matías Galarza, 22, playing MLS football for
  Atlanta United, scoring at the World Cup in under two minutes. Youth from
  an unexpected direction.
- Almirón's last dance: Miguel Almirón back in MLS at Atlanta United after
  Newcastle. Wearing #10 for Paraguay. The career arc of South American
  football in one number.
- Enciso's potential: Julio Enciso born 2004, now at Strasbourg, is the
  player opposing scouts are watching. The winger who can create something
  from nothing.
- Survival mathematics: three points, goal difference -2, Australia also
  on three points. MD3 vs Australia is a direct eliminator for second
  place in Group D.

## Observed Patterns
- Defensive resilience after disastrous OG: the 4-1 scoreline vs USA
  flattered the hosts — Paraguay competed but individual errors compounded
- Counter-punch on the break: the 0-1 vs Turkey showed Alfaro's team can
  execute a defensive setup and score early
- Set piece vulnerability: both goals conceded vs USA came from pressure
  situations in the first 45
- Palmeiras contingent: Sosa, Gustavo Gómez, and Mauricio all from the
  Brazilian powerhouse — shared club identity helps cohesion
- Age gap management: veterans (Balbuena, G. Gómez born 1991/1993) alongside
  teenagers (Maidana born 2005) — Alfaro managing a genuine generation bridge
