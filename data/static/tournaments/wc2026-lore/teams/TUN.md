# Tunisia (TUN)

## Tournament History
7th World Cup appearance (1978, 1998, 2002, 2006, 2018, 2022, 2026).
Best result: Group stage. First African and Arab nation to win a WC
match (beat Mexico 3-1 in 1978). Beat Panama 2-1 in 2018, and
France 1-0 in 2022 (Khazri). Qualified for 2026 with a historic
clean sheet — first team ever to qualify conceding zero goals across
10 matches.

## Current Squad
Coach: Herve Renard (FRA). Appointed June 15, 2026 as interim after
Sabri Lamouchi was sacked following the 5-1 demolition by Sweden.
Lamouchi was the first coach officially fired mid-tournament at a
World Cup (appointed January 2026, five matches, three losses).

Renard is a World Cup specialist: managed Morocco (2018), Saudi Arabia
(2022 — the famous 2-1 upset of Argentina), two AFCON titles with
Zambia (2012) and Ivory Coast (2015). His third World Cup. Brought in
to salvage what he can.
Assistants: Michael Hefele (GER).

Key players: Hannibal Mejbri (#10, MF) Burnley — the creative
fulcrum, former Man United academy. Ellyes Skhiri (#17, MF) Eintracht
Frankfurt — experienced midfield presence. Rani Khedira (#13, MF)
Union Berlin — Sami Khedira's brother, brings Bundesliga steel.
Anis Ben Slimane (#25, MF) Norwich City.

Young talents: Rayan Elloumi (#18, FW) Vancouver Whitecaps — born
September 2007, just 18 years old. One of the youngest players at
this World Cup.
Khalil Ayari (#14, MF) PSG — 21, another bright prospect.
Ismael Gharbi (#11, MF) Augsburg — 22, ex-Barcelona academy.

## Group F Results
- SWE 5-1 TUN (Matchday 1) — Catastrophe. Ayari 7', Isak 30' had
  Tunisia 2-0 down inside half an hour. Rekik 43' gave brief hope
  before Gyokeres, Svanberg, and Ayari again completed the rout.
  Led directly to Lamouchi's sacking.

## Storylines
- The Renard effect: can the man who beat Argentina with Saudi Arabia
  engineer another World Cup miracle? Different circumstances — he
  had months with Saudi, here he has days. But the narrative template
  exists: underdog, backs to wall, everything to play for.
- Lamouchi's toxic exit: reports of dressing room discontent, 5-0
  loss to Belgium in a pre-tournament friendly should have been a
  warning sign. Five matches, three losses, gone.
- The young generation: Elloumi (18), Ayari (21), Gharbi (22),
  Mejbri (23) — this is a squad being built for the future. The
  World Cup might be too soon for some of them.
- Omar Rekik (#4) scored Tunisia's only goal. Brother of Karim Rekik
  (ex-Arsenal, Hertha, Sevilla). Defender scoring from what was
  likely a set piece.
- Historical pedigree: first African/Arab WC winners (1978), beat
  France in 2022. But the 5-1 vs Sweden erased the goodwill.
  Renard's job is to restore the belief that comes from that history.

## Observed Patterns
- Defensively fragile: 5-1 loss exposed structural problems at the
  back. Gyokeres and Isak cut through at will.
- Set piece threat: Rekik's goal suggests aerial ability from dead
  balls, potentially Tunisia's best route to goals.
- Midfield quality exists (Skhiri, Mejbri, Khedira) but was overrun
  by Sweden's intensity and pressing.
- Renard effect unknown: entirely new tactical approach possible in
  48 hours of preparation. Expect defensive reorganization at minimum.
- GK situation: Chamakh (#1) is young (24) and had a difficult debut.
  Dahmen (#16) is the experienced option.
