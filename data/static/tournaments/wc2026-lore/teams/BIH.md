# Bosnia-Herzegovina (BIH)

## Tournament History
2nd World Cup appearance (2014, 2026). The 2014 debut in Brazil was
bittersweet — they won their final group match against Iran but were
eliminated. Twelve years later, a rebuilt squad returns under a coach
who personifies the country's footballing identity. Bosnia has never
progressed past the group stage.

## Current Squad
Coach: Sergej Barbarez (BIH). Legendary former striker — 65 league
goals for HSV, shared 2000-01 Bundesliga top scorer. 47 caps for
Bosnia. Appointed April 2024 with zero coaching experience — and
delivered qualification by knocking out Italy on penalties.
Assistants: Zlatan Bajramovic (BIH — ex-Schalke), Ninoslav Milenkovic
(BIH), Mirko Hrgovic (BIH).

System: 4-2-3-1 / 4-3-3. Direct and physical, relies on Demirovic and
Dzeko's link-up, with runners from midfield.

GK: Nikola Vasilj (#1, St. Pauli — promoted to Bundesliga with St.
Pauli, solid shot-stopper).

Key axis: Ermedin Demirovic (#10, VfB Stuttgart) — the number 10 and
most dangerous creative force, technically gifted, 28 years old.
Edin Dzeko (#11, Schalke 04) — the legend, born 1986-03-17, now 40
years old. Still in the squad. Bosnia's greatest ever goalscorer.
Playing his farewell World Cup at 40. Amar Dedic (#7, Benfica) —
right wing-back or right midfielder, emerging talent. Sead Kolasinac
(#5, Atalanta, born 1993-06-20) — the captain, veteran left-back.

Midfield: Benjamin Tahirovic (#6, Brondby, born 2003-03-03) —
22-year-old rising midfielder. Amir Hadziahmetovic (#16, Hull City)
— the engine. Dzenis Burnic (#17, Karlsruher SC).

Young generation: Tarik Muharemovic (#4, Sassuolo, born 2003-02-28),
Kerim Alajbegovic (#19, RB Salzburg, born 2007-09-21) — just 18,
youngest in the squad. Esmir Bajraktarevic (#20, PSV Eindhoven, born
2005-03-10), Nidal Celik (#24, Sturm Graz, born 2006-07-17).
Mladen Jurkas (#12, GK, FK Borac, born 2007-10-07) — third keeper,
also just 18.

Scorer: Jovo Lukic (#25, Universitatea Cluj) — the man who opened
the scoring against Canada.

## Group B Results
- CAN 1-1 BIH (Matchday 1) — Lukic 21' / Larin 78'. Bosnia's first
  goal of the tournament, Lukic striking early. They held the lead
  until the 78th minute when Larin leveled. A point, but it felt
  like two dropped.
- SUI 4-1 BIH (Matchday 2) — Manzambi 74' 90', Vargas 84',
  Xhaka 90+7' pen / Mahmic 90+3'. A brutal second half. Bosnia
  conceded three times in the final 17 minutes after Mahmic had
  briefly leveled at 1-1. The collapse was total — from potential
  point to 4-1 defeat.

1 pt, GD -3. Must beat Qatar in MD3 to have any hope of advancing,
and require Switzerland to beat Canada simultaneously.

## Storylines
- Edin Dzeko at 40: the greatest Bosnian footballer of all time is
  playing his farewell World Cup at 40 years old, at Schalke 04 in
  the German second tier. He has scored 64 international goals. Every
  minute he's on the pitch is history.
- Barbarez coaching his country: a former Hamburg and Dortmund striker
  now managing the squad he once starred for. The continuity of
  footballing identity — hardworking Balkan diaspora with European
  club quality — runs through the coaching setup.
- The 4-1 collapse: Bosnia led 1-1 against Switzerland in the 90th
  minute and conceded three times. Manzambi's brace and Xhaka's late
  penalty turned a potential point into a heavy defeat. The manner of
  the collapse was alarming.
- A young spine emerging: Tahirovic (22), Muharemovic (23), Dedic (23),
  Bajraktarevic (21), Alajbegovic (18) — Bosnia have genuine future
  stars. The tournament is painful now but the next cycle looks
  interesting.
- The Diaspora squad: almost the entire squad plays their club football
  in Germany, Austria, the Netherlands, and England. Bosnia's talent
  pipeline runs through the Balkan diaspora in Western Europe.

## Observed Patterns
- Strong in the first half hour — the Lukic goal vs Canada showed
  they can execute set piece and early tactical plans
- Second-half defensive fragility: both defeats (near-defeat vs CAN,
  actual defeat vs SUI) came from late concessions under pressure
- Dzeko and Demirovic work better off the ball than on it — they
  need service, and when the midfield is overwhelmed, they go quiet
- Vasilj is a reliable keeper but was helpless against Switzerland's
  late barrage — the defensive structure collapsed around him
- Young players show quality in flashes but tournament intensity
  has exposed gaps in concentration and positional discipline
