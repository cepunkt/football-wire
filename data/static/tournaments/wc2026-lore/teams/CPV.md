# Cape Verde (CPV)

## Tournament History
First ever World Cup appearance. Population ~530,000. Island nation
off the west coast of Africa. Qualified through CAF qualifying — a
remarkable achievement for one of the smallest football nations on
the continent. Following in the footsteps of Curacao as tournament
debutants making history.

## Current Squad
Coach: Pedro Brito "Bubista" (CPV). In charge since 2020. Former
national team captain (28 caps). Named CAF Coach of the Year 2025.
Built this qualification campaign from the ground up.
Assistants: Filipe Neto, Humberto Bettencourt.

System: Defensive, organized, counter-attacking. Built for
resilience. The 0-0 against Spain showed a team that knows its
strengths and refuses to abandon them.

Key players: Logan Costa (#5, DF) Villarreal — the star. La Liga
centre-back, the only player at a top-5 European league. The
defensive anchor. Vozinha (#1, GK) Chaves — born June 1986, 40
YEARS OLD. Heroic against Spain. The oldest player at the tournament.
Stopira (#2, DF) Torreense — born 1988, 38. Another veteran
holding the line.

Diaspora squad: players scattered across Portugal (heavy
representation — 6 players), Turkey, Cyprus, Russia, Bulgaria,
Hungary, Romania, Ireland, Netherlands, USA. The Cape Verdean
football network mirrors the diaspora itself.

Roberto Lopes (#4, DF) Shamrock Rovers — plays in the League of
Ireland. Chose Cape Verde through heritage.
Steven Moreira (#22, DF) Columbus Crew — MLS experience.
CJ dos Santos (#23, GK) San Diego FC — American-born Cape Verdean.

## Group H Results
- ESP 0-0 CPV (Matchday 1) — The result that shook the tournament.
  Cape Verde held Euro 2024 champions Spain to a goalless draw.
  Vozinha was extraordinary in goal. The entire defensive unit was
  disciplined, organized, and fearless. Cape Verde's greatest ever
  football result.

1 pt. Historic.

## Storylines
- David vs Goliath: 600,000 people holding Spain — a country of 47
  million, Euro champions, with Yamal and Rodri — to 0-0. This is
  why the World Cup exists.
- Vozinha at 40: the goalkeeper who denied Spain. Career-defining
  performance. At 40, keeping a clean sheet against the European
  champions. The kind of story that transcends sport.
- The Portugal connection: Portuguese-speaking island nation, heavy
  Portuguese league representation. The cultural and football ties
  are deep.
- Can they do it again? The 0-0 was heroic but Uruguay in MD2 is
  another massive challenge. Bielsa's intensity could be too much.
  But after Spain, nothing is impossible.
- Following Curacao's path: both island nations, both debutants,
  both producing moments that justify their place at the tournament.

## Observed Patterns
- Elite defensive organization: held Spain to 0 goals
- Goalkeeper dependency: Vozinha is the difference between heroics
  and heavy defeats. At 40, can he sustain it?
- Limited attacking threat: creating chances is secondary to not
  conceding. Counter-attacks are the only offensive route.
- Veteran leadership: Vozinha (40), Stopira (38) bring decades of
  experience to the defensive structure
- Set piece defending: well-drilled, disciplined in the box
