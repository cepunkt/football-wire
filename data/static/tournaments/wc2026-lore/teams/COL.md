# Colombia (COL)

## Tournament History
7th World Cup appearance (1962, 1990, 1994, 1998, 2014, 2018, 2026).
Best: Quarter-finals 2014 (James Rodriguez Golden Boot, beat Uruguay,
lost to Brazil 2-1). The Valderrama/Higuita 1990 generation reached
the R16. Copa America 2024 finalists (lost to Argentina 1-0 AET).
This squad arrives with genuine confidence under Lorenzo.

## Current Squad
Coach: Nestor Lorenzo (ARG). Former defender, worked as assistant
to Jose Pekerman for years and inherited the project. Quiet,
methodical, built the team that reached the Copa America 2024 final.
Assistants: Luis Perea (COL — former Atletico Madrid, 73 caps),
Fernando Allocco (ARG).

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Attack-minded. Wide forwards with pace.
Midfield creativity around James Rodriguez when fit. Counter-attacking
capacity combined with technical quality.

GK: David Ospina (#1, Atletico Nacional) — born 1988, 37. Still
the first choice. The veteran keeper who has played in England
(Arsenal, Fulham), Italy (Napoli), and Saudi Arabia. Final career
chapter at his boyhood club back home. Camilo Vargas (#12, Atlas)
as backup.

Defence: Davinson Sanchez (#23, Galatasaray) — the experienced CB,
former Spurs. Yerry Mina (#13, Cagliari) — the physical CB,
former Barcelona and Everton. Daniel Munoz (#2, Crystal Palace) —
SCORED vs Uzbekistan at 40'. The right-back doubling as an
attacking threat. Johan Mojica (#17, Mallorca) — left-back.
Santiago Arias (#4, Independiente) — veteran born 1992, depth.

Midfield: James Rodriguez (#10, Minnesota United) — born 1991, 34.
The 2014 Golden Boot winner. Still the creative reference point.
Whether he starts or impacts from the bench, defenders know exactly
where the danger comes from. Richard Rios (#6, Benfica) — born
2000, the modern engine. Jefferson Lerma (#16, Crystal Palace) —
the destroyer. Juan Fernando Quintero (#20, River Plate) — the
creative alternative.

Attack: Luis Diaz (#7, Bayern Munich) — SCORED vs Uzbekistan at
65'. The star. Left winger who evolved from Porto to Liverpool to
Bayern. The most dangerous Colombian in a generation since Falcao.
Jaminton Campaz (#21, Rosario Central) — SCORED at 90+9'. The
supersub who buried the game. Born May 2000. Cucho Hernandez (#19,
Real Betis) — the explosive forward option. Jhon Cordoba (#9,
Krasnodar) — the physical striker. Colombian Luis Suarez (#25,
Sporting CP) — not the Uruguayan one.

Crystal Palace bloc: Munoz (#2) and Lerma (#16) — two Palace players
at the heart of Colombia's structure.

## Group K Results
- COL 3-1 UZB (Matchday 1) — Daniel Munoz scored at 40', Uzbekistan
  equalized through Fayzullaev at 60', then Luis Diaz restored the
  lead at 65' and Campaz completed the win at 90+9'. Three goals,
  three scorers. The lead, the wobble, the reassertion. Copa America
  finalists reminding everyone why they're favourites here.

3 pts. Top of Group K. Convincing enough despite the wobble.

## Storylines
- Luis Diaz's trajectory: Port-of-Spain to Porto to Liverpool to
  Bayern Munich. The winger from La Guajira, Colombia's Caribbean
  coast, is now playing Champions League football at the biggest club
  in Germany. When he scored at 65', it was the goal that ended
  Uzbekistan's resistance.
- James at 34: Colombia's most globally recognized player of his
  generation, now at Minnesota United in MLS, still wearing the 10.
  The 2014 World Cup Golden Boot winner's last dance on the biggest
  stage.
- Campaz at 90+9': Jaminton Campaz, playing his club football in
  Argentina at Rosario Central, came on and scored in the 99th minute
  to kill the game. The kind of super-sub moment that builds World Cup
  mythology.
- Munoz's overlap: the Crystal Palace right-back scored the opening
  goal. Colombia's full-backs as attacking weapons is a feature of
  Lorenzo's system, not an accident.
- Copa America 2024 finalists: Colombia reached the Copa final,
  losing only to Argentina. The players know what it feels like to
  go deep in a tournament. That experience compounds.

## Observed Patterns
- Full-backs involved in attack — Munoz especially as an additional
  forward option
- Luis Diaz's pace and directness is the primary threat; opponents
  must account for him above all others
- Vulnerable to equalization after taking the lead — Uzbekistan
  leveled at 60' after Colombia led — game management question
- James Rodriguez's positioning and through-ball range opens spaces
  that simpler midfielders cannot
- Campaz as a late-game impact player — comfort with supersub role
  is structural, not accidental
