# Saudi Arabia (KSA)

## Tournament History
7th World Cup appearance (1994, 1998, 2002, 2006, 2018, 2022, 2026).
Best result: Round of 16 (1994). The 2022 team beat Argentina 2-1 in
one of the greatest World Cup upsets ever — Al-Dawsari's 53' solo run
and curled finish after beating two defenders. Renard's tactical
masterclass, the green wall of noise. That result changed Saudi
football's global perception overnight.

## Current Squad
Coach: Georgios Donis (GRE). Appointed April 2026, succeeding Renard
just weeks before the tournament. Greek tactician — defensive
organization, structured approach.
Assistants: Makis Angelinas (GRE).

System: Compact 4-4-2 / 4-2-3-1. Counter-attacking built around
pace and directness. The Saudi Pro League's quality has improved with
investment, but the national team still relies on organization and
collective effort over individual brilliance.

Key players: Salem Al-Dawsari (#10, FW) Al Hilal — the 2022 hero.
That goal against Argentina. 34 years old now, still the creative
heartbeat. Saleh Al-Shehri (#11, FW) Al Ittihad — scored vs
Argentina in 2022 too. The strike partnership carries national
mythology. Nasser Al-Dawsari (#6, MF) Al Hilal — the midfield
engine, no relation to Salem.

Entirely Saudi Pro League squad except Saud Abdulhamid (#12, DF)
at RC Lens — the only European-based player. The domestic league IS
the national team.

Al Hilal core: Ali Lajami, Al-Tambakti, N. Al-Dawsari, S. Al-Dawsari,
Kanno, Mandash, Al-Harbi — 7 Hilal players.

## Group H Results
- KSA 1-1 URU (Matchday 1) — Al-Amri 41' header for KSA, Araujo
  80' equalizer for Uruguay. Saudi Arabia led for 39 minutes against
  a Bielsa team featuring Valverde and Nunez. A point earned through
  defensive discipline.

1 pt. Competitive.

## Storylines
- The 2022 echo: can they produce another upset? Beat Argentina then,
  drew with Uruguay now. Saudi Arabia at World Cups are unpredictable
  and dangerous when organized.
- Al-Dawsari at 34: this is his last World Cup. The most famous Saudi
  footballer ever. If he produces another moment, it cements his
  legacy forever.
- The domestic-only model: 25 of 26 players in the Saudi Pro League.
  The investment in the league (Ronaldo, Benzema attracted stars)
  hasn't directly translated to the national team — these are Saudi
  players, not the imported stars.
- Renard managed Saudi Arabia in 2022 — now he manages Tunisia, who
  Saudi could meet if results align. The connections are everywhere.
- Spain in MD2: another giant, another opportunity for an upset. The
  2022 Argentina template is carved into this team's DNA.

## Observed Patterns
- Organized defensive block — led Uruguay for 39 minutes
- Set piece threat: Al-Amri's goal was a header from a set piece
- Counter-attacking pace through Al-Shehri and the wide players
- Late-game vulnerability: conceded at 80' to lose the lead
- Collective over individual — no superstars, just structure
