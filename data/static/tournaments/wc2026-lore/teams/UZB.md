# Uzbekistan (UZB)

## Tournament History
FIRST EVER World Cup appearance. Uzbekistan has never been to a World
Cup as an independent nation. The Soviet Union qualified, but the
Republic of Uzbekistan — independent since 1991 — arrives in 2026
for the first time. Third in the AFC final qualifying round, making
history. The entire country is at a World Cup for the first time.

## Current Squad
Coach: Fabio Cannavaro (ITA). One of the most decorated defenders
in history — 2006 World Cup winner, Ballon d'Or, 136 Italy caps,
Real Madrid, Juventus. Now managing Uzbekistan. His brother Paolo
Cannavaro (#... ) is on his coaching staff. Two Cannavaroes on the
bench at Uzbekistan's first World Cup. There is no stranger, more
wonderful story in this tournament.
Assistants: Eugenio Albarella (ITA), Francesco Troise (ITA), Paolo
Cannavaro (ITA — Fabio's brother).

System: Organized defensive structure with quick transitions. A mix
of domestic Uzbek league players and diaspora footballers playing
in Iran, UAE, and Turkey. Cannavaro has built defensive discipline
(as one might expect) as the foundation.

GK: Utkir Yusupov (#1, Navbahor Namangan) — domestic based. The
first-choice custodian for historic duty.

Defence: Abdukodir Khusanov (#2, Manchester City) — born February
2004, 22. At MANCHESTER CITY. The most high-profile Uzbek player
in history. Rustam Ashurmatov (#5, Esteghlal Tehran) — the
experienced CB. Farrukh Sayfiev (#4, FK Neftchi) — veteran born
1991, anchoring the back line. Bekhruz Karimov (#24, Surkhon FK)
— born August 2007, 18. Youngest in the squad.

Midfield: Abbosbek Fayzullaev (#22, Basaksehir) — born October
2003, 22. SCORED vs Colombia at 60'. The goal-scorer of the moment
that briefly made it 1-1. The most technical of Uzbekistan's
midfielders. Jaloliddin Masharipov (#10, Esteghlal Tehran) — the
creative centre. Oston Urunov (#11, Persepolis) — engine in
transition. Sherzod Esanov (#23, FK Buxoro) — born February 2003,
23. Two midfielders born in 2003 at the first World Cup.

Attack: Eldor Shomurodov (#14, Basaksehir) — the senior striker,
ex-Roma and Genoa, 31. Most experienced Uzbek forward in European
football history. Igor Sergeev (#21, Persepolis) — the backup option.
Azizbek Amonov (#20, Dinamo Samarkand) — domestic based forward.

Uzbek domestic presence: 8 players from Uzbek leagues (Pakhtakor,
Navbahor, Neftchi, Nasaf, FK Buxoro, Dinamo Samarkand). A genuine
national team, not purely a diaspora squad.

## Group K Results
- UZB 1-3 COL (Matchday 1) — Fayzullaev equalized at 60' making it
  1-1 after Munoz scored for Colombia at 40'. Then Luis Diaz scored
  at 65' and Campaz at 90+9' completed the rout. Uzbekistan competed
  for an hour and had an answer to Colombia's opening goal. The
  concession pattern — level at 60' and then unraveling — tells
  the story of experience vs ambition.

0 pts. Historical debut ends in defeat. But they scored.

## Storylines
- The Cannavaro brothers: Fabio (World Cup winner, Ballon d'Or) and
  Paolo (former Napoli captain, 7 Italy caps) — both on the touchline
  at Uzbekistan's first ever World Cup. Nothing in football coaching
  is stranger or more charming.
- Khusanov at Manchester City: Abdukodir Khusanov, born in Uzbekistan
  in 2004, plays Champions League football with Pep Guardiola at Man
  City. The global reach of the Premier League deposited a World Cup
  debutant's star player in Manchester. Cannavaro built his defence
  around a City player.
- Fayzullaev's equalizer: for 25 minutes after the 60th minute, it
  was 1-1 against Copa America runners-up Colombia. The 22-year-old
  Basaksehir midfielder scored and Uzbekistan were level at a World
  Cup. History was happening. Then Diaz and Campaz buried it.
- First ever World Cup: everything is history. The flag in the
  stadium, the anthem, the first kick. A nation of 36 million people
  watching football's biggest stage for the first time.
- The Iranian connection: Ashurmatov, Masharipov, Hamrobekov,
  Urunov, Sergeev — five players based in the Iranian Pro League,
  reflecting Central Asia's football geography.

## Observed Patterns
- Defensively organized but not impenetrable — Colombia's quality
  eventually told
- Fayzullaev as the primary creative and dangerous midfield threat
- Shomurodov's movement is familiar from European leagues —
  a recognizable focal point
- Karimov's 18-year-old presence signals long-term ambition over
  short-term pragmatism
- Energy and directness on transitions, worn down in final quarter
  against technical opponents
