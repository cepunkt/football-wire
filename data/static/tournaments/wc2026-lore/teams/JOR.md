# Jordan (JOR)

## Tournament History
First ever World Cup appearance. 2023 Asian Cup finalists — lost to
Qatar in the final. That run announced Jordan as a serious Asian
football nation. Population 11 million. Qualified through Asian
qualifying — a historic achievement for a country where football
competes with basketball for national attention.

## Current Squad
Coach: Jamal Sellami (MAR). Moroccan coach — part of a growing trend
of North African coaches managing West Asian teams. Experienced in
Arab football.
Assistants: Mustapha Khalfi, Karim Elidrissi, Omar Najhi.

Key players: Musa Al-Taamari (#10, FW) Stade Rennais — the star.
Ligue 1 quality, technical, creative. Jordan's most talented player
and the only one at a top-5 European league. Everything creative
runs through him.
Ali Olwan (#9, FW) Al Sailiya — scored against Austria (50'). The
striker. Odeh Al-Fakhouri (#11, FW) Pyramids — born November 2005,
20. The young talent.

Regional base: players spread across Jordan (Al Hussein, Al Faisaly,
Al Wahdat), Iraq (Al Zawra'a, Al Karma, Al-Quwa Al-Jawiya), Qatar,
Malaysia, Saudi Arabia. The squad reflects Jordan's position at the
crossroads of West Asian football.

Al Hussein SC core: 6 players from one Jordanian club. The domestic
backbone.

Yazan Al-Arab (#5, DF) FC Seoul — the own goal scorer vs Austria.
Plays in the K-League.

## Group J Results
- AUT 3-1 JOR (Matchday 1) — Olwan 50' equalized after Austria led
  through Schmid 21'. Then Al-Arab OG 76' and Arnautovic 90'+12'
  penalty. Jordan competed for 75 minutes. The equalizer showed they
  belong. The collapse showed the quality gap.

0 pts. Facing Algeria next.

## Storylines
- The Asian Cup finalists at a World Cup: Jordan's 2023 run to the
  Asian Cup final was the greatest achievement in Jordanian football
  history. The World Cup qualification topped it. Now they're here
  and competing.
- Olwan's equalizer: Jordan led Austria for 26 minutes. A genuine
  World Cup moment for a nation experiencing all of this for the
  first time. The goal matters regardless of the final score.
- Al-Taamari as the creative engine: Rennes' midfielder is the
  quality difference-maker. If Jordan are to get a result against
  Algeria, he's the man.
- The own goal and the collapse: from 1-1 at 75' to 1-3. The late
  penalty at 90'+12' was harsh. Jordan can compete but not for 90
  minutes against quality opposition.
- First World Cup experience: everything is new. The crowd, the
  stage, the intensity. MD2 vs Algeria is a genuine opportunity.

## Observed Patterns
- Competitive for long periods but fade in the final 15 minutes
- Counter-attacking through Al-Taamari's creativity
- Defensive organization but vulnerable to set pieces and sustained
  pressure
- Disciplinary control good (no cards crisis)
- The quality gap appears late in matches when fitness drops
