# France (FRA)

## Tournament History
17th World Cup appearance. Winners in 1998 (3-0 vs Brazil) and 2018
(4-2 vs Croatia). Runners-up in 2006 and 2022 (both lost on penalties).
Four finals total. A nation that cannot stop reaching them.

## Current Squad
Coach: Didier Deschamps (FRA). Already one of only three men to win
the World Cup as player (1998 captain) and coach (2018) — alongside
Zagallo and Beckenbauer. Going for a second title as coach at his
farewell tournament. Will stand down after 2026.
Assistant: Guy Stephan (FRA) — longtime Deschamps lieutenant, every
campaign together.

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Built around Mbappé's movement and Tchouaméni's
control. Deschamps trusts the established core. Rotation is managed,
not aggressive.

GK: Mike Maignan (#16, AC Milan) — the starter, one of the best in Europe.
Brice Samba (#1, Rennes) first choice by number but not in practice.
Robin Risser (#23, Lens) born 2004 — the future in waiting.

Defence: Jules Koundé (#5, Barcelona) — right back or CB, elite. William
Saliba (#17, Arsenal) — born 2001, the wall. Dayot Upamecano (#4, Bayern).
Ibrahima Konaté (#15, Liverpool). Lucas Hernandez (#21, PSG). Théo
Hernandez (#19, Al Hilal) — the two Hernandez brothers, both internationals.
Malo Gusto (#2, Chelsea) born 2003. Lucas Digne (#3, Aston Villa).

Midfield: Aurélien Tchouaméni (#8, Real Madrid) — the anchor, the future
Ballon d'Or candidate. N'Golo Kanté (#13, Fenerbahçe) born 1991 —
returning at 34, still elite. Manu Koné (#6, Roma) born 2001. Adrien
Rabiot (#14, AC Milan). Warren Zaïre-Emery (#18, PSG) born 2006 — the
youngest starter candidate, PSG academy's finest product.

Attack: Kylian Mbappé (#10, Real Madrid) — the captain, the mission.
Brace vs Senegal (66', 90+6'). 27 years old, World Cup finalist at 19,
winner at 22. Marcus Thuram (#9, Inter Milan) — the number nine. Ousmane
Dembélé (#7, PSG). Bradley Barcola (#12, PSG) — scored vs Senegal (82'),
the new France starter. Michael Olise (#11, Bayern) — born 2001, the
outstanding pacy option. Désiré Doué (#20, PSG) born 2005. Rayan Cherki
(#24, Man City) born 2003. Jean-Philippe Mateta (#22, Crystal Palace).

PSG contingent: Mbappé (ex), Dembélé, Barcola, Zaïre-Emery, L. Hernandez,
Doué — the club of the squad.

## Storylines
- Deschamps chasing history: player 1998, coach 2018, potentially coach
  2026. Only Zagallo (Brazil, 1958/1970) and Beckenbauer (West Germany,
  1974/1990) have won the World Cup as both. Deschamps is one tournament
  from joining that list.
- Mbappé the captain: 2022 final hat trick still raw in memory, captain
  of France and Real Madrid. If France win, he becomes the defining player
  of his era. Already scoring in the opening match.
- Barcola's emergence: Bradley Barcola, PSG's explosive left winger, scored
  in just his third major tournament match. The youth movement continues
  even in the most experienced squads.
- Zaïre-Emery at 19: PSG's midfielder born 2006 is the youngest regular
  in Deschamps' plans. The pipeline never stops.
- Kanté's return: N'Golo Kanté at 34, playing in Turkey with Fenerbahçe,
  recalled to the national squad for a World Cup. His work rate is
  unreduced. Some things don't diminish.

## Observed Patterns
- Controlled possession, patient build-up — not spectacular but effective
- Mbappé as a second-half weapon: his two goals vs Senegal came at 66'
  and 90+6' — the deeper the match, the more dangerous he becomes
- Barcola's wing directness: his goal was a result of direct running at
  the defender, unlike France's usual combination play
- Defensive depth: Koundé, Saliba, Upamecano, Konaté — four elite CBs
  and fullbacks means almost no drop-off in personnel
- Deschamps' pragmatism: leads at 66', doesn't throw men forward, trusts
  the structure and lets Mbappé finish the job late
