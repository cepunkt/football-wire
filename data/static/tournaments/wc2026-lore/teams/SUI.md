# Switzerland (SUI)

## Tournament History
13th World Cup appearance (6th consecutive since 2006). Best result:
quarter-finals (1934, 1938, 1954). Under Petkovic they beat France on
penalties at Euro 2020 (QF). At WC 2022 under Yakin they lost to
Portugal 1-6 in the Round of 16. Under Yakin the identity is
unchanged: organized, hard to beat, technically underrated.

## Current Squad
Coach: Murat Yakin (SUI). Former international, Swiss coach now in
his second major tournament at the helm. Methodical, trusts experience
but willing to play young players in key moments. The Manzambi
double against Bosnia came from a player born in 2005.
Assistant: Davide Calla (SUI).

System: 4-2-3-1 / 4-3-3. Well-organized defensively, with Xhaka
directing from deep, midfield runners like Sow and Freuler providing
the engine, and Embolo leading the line.

GK: Gregor Kobel (#1, Borussia Dortmund) — one of the top keepers
in the Bundesliga. Solid, commanding. Yvon Mvogo (#12, Lorient) backup.

Captain: Granit Xhaka (#10, Sunderland AFC) — born 1992-09-27,
now 33. The heartbeat of Switzerland for over a decade. Ex-Arsenal,
now at Sunderland. Scored the 90+7' penalty vs Bosnia to seal the win.
His leadership and dead ball delivery define the Swiss system.

Key players: Breel Embolo (#7, Stade Rennais) — the striker, born
in Cameroon, raised in Switzerland. Physical, target and runner.
Scored the 17th-minute penalty vs Qatar. Manuel Akanji (#5, Inter
Milan) — elite centre-back, Champions League winner. Dan Ndoye (#11,
Nottingham Forest) — dynamic winger. Ruben Vargas (#17, Sevilla) —
scored vs Bosnia 84'. Ardon Jashari (#14, AC Milan) — rising
central midfielder. Remo Freuler (#8, Bologna) — veteran utility
midfielder, 13 international seasons.

Young stars: Johan Manzambi (#9, SC Freiburg, born 2005-10-14) —
20 years old, scored TWICE vs Bosnia coming on as impact sub. The
revelation of the group stage. Aurele Amenda (#24, Eintracht Frankfurt,
born 2003-07-31), Luca Jaquez (#25, VfB Stuttgart, born 2003-06-02),
Fabian Rieder (#22, Augsburg, born 2002-02-16). Marvin Keller (#21,
Young Boys, born 2002-07-03).

Veterans: Ricardo Rodriguez (#13, Real Betis, born 1992-08-25) —
veteran left-back, 11th year in the squad. Silvan Widmer (#3, Mainz).

The OG scorer: Miro Muheim (#2, Hamburger SV) — scored the own goal
that gifted Qatar their equalizer in the 90+4'.

## Group B Results
- QAT 1-1 SUI (Matchday 1) — Embolo 17' pen / Muheim OG 90+4'.
  Switzerland led from the 17th minute through Embolo's penalty and
  looked set for three points. A Muheim own goal in the 90+4' turned
  win into draw. A point wasted. Muheim will not forget that night.
- SUI 4-1 BIH (Matchday 2) — Manzambi 74' 90', Vargas 84',
  Xhaka 90+7' pen / Mahmic 90+3'. After a tight match through 73
  minutes, substitute Manzambi (born 2005!) opened the scoring.
  Bosnia equalized briefly in the 90th, but Vargas and then Manzambi
  again, followed by Xhaka's penalty in stoppage time, turned it into
  a rout. The most goals Switzerland have ever scored in a World Cup
  group match.

4 pts, GD +3. MD3 vs Canada for group leadership. A win takes them
through as group winners. A draw may be enough depending on other
results.

## Storylines
- Manzambi's emergence: a 20-year-old born in 2005 scored twice as
  a substitute against Bosnia. Johan Manzambi (Freiburg) is now the
  story of the Swiss group stage. If Yakin had the courage to start
  him in MD3, the Xhaka-Manzambi axis could be special.
- The Muheim moment: Switzerland were 45 minutes from three points
  vs Qatar when Muheim put the ball in his own net in the 90+4'.
  Football cruelty. It turned a perfect start into a drawn first
  match and means they enter MD3 level with Canada.
- Xhaka the captain-closer: after all the Switzerland goals to seal
  the win vs Bosnia, Xhaka stepped up and converted the 90+7' penalty.
  The oldest and most experienced voice in the dressing room delivering
  under pressure. His 13th year in the national team.
- The Swiss system as identity: Akanji at centre-back, Freuler
  recycling, Xhaka directing, Embolo pressing — the structure is
  the same across competitions and eras. Yakin inherits Petkovic's
  blueprint and refined it rather than reinvented it.
- MD3 vs Canada: both teams on 4 pts, Switzerland GD +3 vs Canada
  GD +6. Switzerland need to win to top the group; Canada need a
  draw. The tactical tension sets up a fascinating finale.

## Observed Patterns
- Embolo as a physical reference point: his penalty vs Qatar and
  general pressing set the tempo. When he's off the ball for Switzerland,
  the attack loses its vertical threat
- Late game intensity is a Swiss weapon — three of their four goals
  vs Bosnia came after the 84th minute; Manzambi as the game-changer
- Xhaka on penalties and set pieces: his delivery from dead balls and
  composure from the spot make him priceless in tight matches
- Akanji and Elvedi as a centre-back pair are defensively elite;
  the 4-1 scoreline masked a solid defensive base for 73 minutes
- Risk-averse in possession — Switzerland do not waste chances but
  also do not dominate games; they tend to win through moments rather
  than territorial control
