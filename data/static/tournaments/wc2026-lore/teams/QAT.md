# Qatar (QAT)

## Tournament History
2nd World Cup appearance (2022 as hosts, 2026). First qualified on
merit for 2026 (beat UAE 2-1, October 2025). The 2022 home tournament
was the worst host performance in WC history — lost all three group
games without winning, 0 points. Now they travel as genuine visitors
for the first time.

## Current Squad
Coach: Julen Lopetegui (ESP). The Spaniard who managed Porto,
Spain (briefly — sacked on the eve of 2018 WC), Real Madrid,
Sevilla (won Europa League twice), and West Ham. Now rebuilding
Qatar's identity after 2022. Ambitious appointment — a European
tactician hired to impose structure on a Gulf League squad.
Assistants: Juan Cruz Ochoa (ESP), Pablo Sanz Iniesta (ESP),
Oscar Caro (ESP).

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Lopetegui's characteristic compact block
with quick transitions. Trying to impose European positional
principles on a squad that plays together domestically.

GK: Meshaal Barsham (#22, Al Sadd) — the senior keeper from 2022.
Mahmud Abunada (#1, Al Rayyan) also in squad.

Key players: Akram Afif (#11, Al Sadd) — the star, top scorer in
the 2022 World Cup qualifiers, technical left-footed attacker.
Hassan Al-Haydos (#10, Al Sadd) — the captain, veteran of multiple
Asian Cups, born 1990-12-11. Almoez Ali (#19, Al Duhail) — the
striker who scored a famous bicycle kick goal in 2019 AFC Asian Cup
final. Edmilson Junior (#8, Al Duhail) — Brazilian-born naturalized
forward. Pedro Miguel (#2, Al Sadd) — Portuguese-born left-back,
born 1990-08-06.

Domestic core: almost the entire squad plays in the Qatar Stars
League — Al Sadd, Al Rayyan, Al Duhail, Al Gharafa dominate the
club affiliations. Homam Ahmed (#14, Cultural Leonesa) is the only
player at a European club.

Young: Ayoub Al-Oui (#13, Al Gharafa, born 2005-03-11), Tahsin
Jamshid (#24, Al Duhail, born 2006-06-16) — both in the squad as
future development selections.

The OG scorer: Mohamed Manai (#26, Al Shamal) — scored the own goal
vs Canada at 75'.

## Group B Results
- QAT 1-1 SUI (Matchday 1) — Muheim OG 90+4' / Embolo 17' pen.
  Qatar held Switzerland at 0-1 until the 90th minute, then a
  desperate Muheim own goal in the 90+4' gave them an unlikely
  equalizer. A genuine steal of a point.
- CAN 6-0 QAT (Matchday 2) — Larin 16', David 29' 45+3' 90+2',
  Saliba 64', Manai OG 75'. A humbling defeat. Canada's press was
  relentless and Qatar had no answer. The gulf in class was exposed
  across 90 minutes without mercy.

1 pt, GD -6. Eliminated unless dramatic results occur in MD3.

## Storylines
- From hosts to travelers: Qatar hosted 2022, went out in the group
  stage as the worst host nation performance in World Cup history.
  Now they're playing away in North America. Lopetegui was hired to
  build something different. The 6-0 vs Canada suggests the gap to
  the elite remains enormous.
- Lopetegui's appointment: bringing in a top European manager to
  coach Qatar was a statement of intent. He has a track record of
  building solid defensive structures — but the 6-0 raises questions
  about whether European tactical concepts can be imposed quickly
  enough on a domestically-focused squad.
- The 90+4' equalizer vs Switzerland: Qatar's most dramatic moment
  of the tournament. Trailing 1-0 in the 90th minute, a Muheim own
  goal gave them a point they barely deserved. A moment of fortune
  that gave their campaign a heartbeat.
- The 6-0 reality check: Canada's pressing game exposed Qatar's
  limitations with brutal efficiency. Six goals conceded, own goals,
  David's hat trick. The scoreline was unforgiving.
- The domestic squad problem: almost every player competes in the
  Qatar Stars League against each other week to week. The level
  gap when meeting CONCACAF or European sides at a World Cup is
  the central structural challenge Lopetegui cannot solve overnight.

## Observed Patterns
- Organized defensively in structured phases but unable to cope
  with high tempo pressing from mobile opponents
- Akram Afif is the only player who consistently threatens in
  transition — the team's danger is entirely channeled through him
- Al-Haydos provides experience and set piece quality but his
  age (35) limits his high-intensity output
- The squad's cohesion from playing together domestically doesn't
  translate to effectiveness against teams with higher individual
  quality
- Lopetegui's influence visible in defensive shape but hasn't yet
  unlocked an attacking identity to complement it
