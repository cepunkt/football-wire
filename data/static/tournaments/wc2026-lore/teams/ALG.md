# Algeria (ALG)

## Tournament History
5th World Cup appearance (1982, 1986, 2010, 2014, 2026). Best:
Round of 16 (2014 — lost to Germany in extra time). The 2014
generation (Slimani, Brahimi, Feghouli) pushed Germany hard. Now a
new generation with European pedigree arrives 12 years later.

## Current Squad
Coach: Vladimir Petkovic (SUI/BIH). Appointed February 2024. Managed
Switzerland for 7 years (2014-2021) — beat France on penalties in
Euro 2020 R16, reached QF. Experienced, organized, European structure.
Assistants: Davide Morandi, Nabil Neghiz (ALG).

Key players: Riyad Mahrez (#7, FW) Al Ahli — still the star name
at 35, though at a Saudi club now. Ex-Man City, Premier League
champion. The creative reference point. Houssem Aouar (#8, MF)
Al Ittihad — ex-Lyon prodigy, also in Saudi.
Rayan Ait-Nouri (#15, DF) Manchester City — the best young talent.
Moved from Wolves to City. Left-back with attacking quality.
Ramy Bensebaini (#21, DF) Borussia Dortmund — experienced and
reliable. Mohamed Amoura (#18, FW) Wolfsburg — pace and directness.

Emerging: Ibrahim Maza (#22, MF) Bayer Leverkusen — born November
2005, 20. The most exciting Algerian prospect in years.
Anis Hadj Moussa (#11, FW) Feyenoord — quick, direct winger.
Fares Chaibi (#10, MF) Frankfurt — creative playmaker.

GK: Luca Zidane (#23, GK) Granada — yes, THAT Zidane. Zinedine's
son. Born in France, eligible through Algerian heritage.

## Group J Results
- ARG 3-0 ALG (Matchday 1) — Messi hat trick. Algeria had no answer
  to the defending champions. Completely outclassed. Petkovic's
  organization couldn't contain Messi in the channels.

0 pts. Need results.

## Storylines
- Messi happened: sometimes you just run into the GOAT on his day.
  Three goals, no response. Algeria's tournament begins from here.
- The Bundesliga connection: Ait-Nouri (City), Bensebaini (Dortmund),
  Amoura (Wolfsburg), Maza (Leverkusen), Chaibi (Frankfurt) — five
  players in German football. Quality exists.
- Luca Zidane in goal: the surname carries weight. If he plays and
  performs, it's a story. The Zidane lineage at a World Cup.
- Mahrez at 35: the 2014 generation's last man standing. This is
  his farewell too. Can he produce one more moment?
- Recovery needed: MD2 vs Jordan is must-win. The talent is there
  but the Messi result could damage confidence.

## Observed Patterns
- Organizational structure from Petkovic but couldn't handle elite
  attacking quality
- European-based players provide technical foundation
- Amoura and Hadj Moussa offer pace on the counter
- Defensive vulnerability against top-tier opposition
- Mental recovery from 0-3 is the immediate challenge
