# New Zealand (NZL)

## Tournament History
3rd World Cup appearance (1982, 2010, 2026). The All Whites. 2010
was the miracle — drew all three group games (including Italy 1-1)
and went home unbeaten but eliminated. 44 years between their first
and third World Cups. Oceania's perpetual nearly-men.

## Current Squad
Coach: Darren Bazeley (NZL). Domestic appointment, steady development.
Assistants: Tony Readings, Simon Elliott (NZL — former All Whites
midfielder, 66 caps).

Key players: Chris Wood (#9, FW) Nottingham Forest — the star. NZ's
all-time top scorer. Premier League proven, physical, clinical in the
air. The target man everything is built around.
Elijah Just (#11, MF) Motherwell — the revelation. Scored twice
against Iran (7', 54'). Scottish Premiership player who arrived at
the World Cup and immediately delivered. 26 years old, breakout
tournament.
Liberato Cacace (#13, DF) Wrexham — left-back with attacking instinct.
Tyler Bindon (#4, DF) Sheffield United — born January 2005, 21.
The youngest and most talented defender.

Lower-league depth: the squad spans from the Premier League (Wood)
to the A-League (Bayliss), New Zealand domestic league (Payne, de
Vries, Singh, Rufer), and English lower divisions. Tommy Smith
(#26, DF) plays for Braintree Town in the National League South —
the lowest-league player at the World Cup.

GK: Max Crocombe (#1) Millwall — English Championship. Solid,
experienced at 32.

## Group G Results
- IRN 2-2 NZL (Matchday 1) — Just 7' 54' (brace) gave NZ the lead
  twice. Rezaeian 32', Mohebbi 64' equalized both times for Iran.
  New Zealand were the better team for long periods. Two leads
  thrown away, but 2 goals and a point against an Asian powerhouse
  is a statement.

1 pt. More than expected.

## Storylines
- Elijah Just's World Cup: two goals in his first World Cup match.
  A Motherwell player scoring twice at a World Cup. Football's
  romance at its finest.
- Chris Wood as the focal point: everything goes through the Forest
  striker. If Wood is isolated, NZ create nothing. If the service
  is good, he's a handful for any defence.
- The 2010 echo: drew all three games unbeaten but went out. Can this
  generation do better? The Iran draw suggests they can compete.
- Tommy Smith at Braintree Town: the gap between the lowest and
  highest league levels in one squad. Non-league to World Cup.
- Two leads thrown away: the mental fragility of being ahead and not
  being able to close. Sounds like a team not used to leading at
  this level.

## Observed Patterns
- Fast starters: Just scored at 7'. NZ came out aggressive.
- Cannot close games: led twice, drew. Protecting leads is the
  weakness.
- Physical, direct play — Wood in the air, Just running in behind
- Defensive vulnerability: Rezaeian and Mohebbi found space to
  equalize. The back line is the weak point.
- Energy and effort compensate for quality gap
