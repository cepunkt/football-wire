# Mexico (MEX)

## Tournament History
18th World Cup appearance — one of the most of any nation. Best:
Quarter-finals (1970, 1986 — both as hosts). The "quinto partido"
curse: eliminated in the Round of 16 in seven consecutive World Cups
(1994-2022). Every Mexican generation grows up with the weight of
that ceiling. Co-hosts in 2026 alongside USA and Canada.

## Current Squad
Coach: Javier Aguirre (MEX). Third spell as Mexico manager. The
ultimate pragmatist — results over style. Known as "El Vasco."
Assistants: Rafael Marquez (MEX — legendary CB, 5 World Cups as
player, now coaching), Antonio Amor Fernandez (ESP).

System: Compact 4-3-3 / 4-4-2. Defensive organization first,
counter-attacking transitions. Aguirre's Mexico are efficient, not
exciting. The Azteca does the entertaining.

Key players: Raul Jimenez (#9, FW) Fulham — 35 years old, first WC
goal in the opener dedicated to his late father. Skull fracture
survivor. The emotional heart of this squad. Santiago Gimenez (#11,
FW) AC Milan — the future striker, clinical finisher. Edson Alvarez
(#4, DF) Fenerbahce — defensive anchor, former Ajax/West Ham.
Erik Lira (#6, MF) Cruz Azul — the presser, created the opening goal
through sheer intensity. Julian Quinones (#16, FW) Al Qadsiah —
scored the tournament's first goal.

Veteran presence: Guillermo Ochoa (#13, GK) — 40 YEARS OLD. AEL
Limassol. Six World Cups if you count 2006 as backup. Mexico's
all-time most capped player. On the bench but still there.

Young talent: Gilberto Mora (#19, MF) Club Tijuana — born October
2008, just 17 years old. One of the youngest players in World Cup
history. Obed Vargas (#18, MF) Atletico Madrid — 20, already at a
top European club.

Guadalajara core: Rangel, Romo, Alvarado, Gonzalez, Gutierrez — 5
players from Chivas. The domestic backbone.

## Storylines
- Co-host advantage: Mexico play at Azteca (2,240m altitude, 80,000+
  capacity). The fortress effect is real — South Africa were
  intimidated before kickoff.
- The Jimenez redemption: skull fracture November 2020, career nearly
  over. Five and a half years later, scoring at a World Cup in the
  Azteca. Dedicated to his late father. The tournament's first great
  human story.
- Marquez on the touchline: five World Cups as a player, now coaching.
  The passing of the torch made literal.
- Ochoa's sixth World Cup: 40 years old, on the bench, still selected.
  Living legend status.
- Can they break the quinto partido? Seven consecutive Round of 16
  exits. This generation has the home advantage none before them had.
- Mora at 17: born 2008. If he plays, he writes history.

## Observed Patterns
- Defensive solidity: 0 goals conceded in 2 matches

- Azteca as 12th man: atmosphere and altitude create hostile

- Aguirre's pragmatism: no unnecessary risks, control the game,
