# DR Congo (COD)

## Tournament History
2nd World Cup appearance (as Zaire 1974, as DR Congo 2026). 52-year
gap — longest of any 2026 qualifier. Zaire in 1974 were the first
Sub-Saharan African WC team: lost all three (Yugoslavia 9-0, Scotland
2-0, Brazil 3-0), the Ilunga Mwepu free kick incident is folklore.
Qualified for 2026 via inter-confederation playoff vs Jamaica (1-0
AET, Tuanzebe goal, 31 March 2026).

## Current Squad
Coach: Sebastien Desabre (FRA). The French coach who has made African
football his career — clubs and national teams across the continent.
Pragmatic, organized, built a squad from the Congolese diaspora
scattered across European leagues.
Assistants: Pamphile Mihayo (COD), Rafael Hamidi.

System: Compact defensive structure with dangerous attacking outlets.
The squad is built around diaspora players who chose DR Congo over
France, Belgium, or other European nations. European-quality individual
talent in a collective structure.

GK: Lionel Mpasi (#1, Le Havre) — the first-choice. Timothy Fayulu
(#16, FC Noah Armenia) and Matthieu Epolo (#21, Standard Liege) as
backups. Epolo born January 2005, 21 — one to watch.

Defence: Aaron Wan-Bissaka (#2, West Ham) — former Man United right-
back, the defensive anchor on the right side. Chancel Mbemba (#22,
Lille) — experienced CB. Arthur Masuaku (#26, RC Lens) —
left-back. Axel Tuanzebe (#4, Burnley) — the physical option at
centre-back.

Midfield: Ngal'ayel Mukau (#6, Lille) — born November 2004, 21.
Young engine in the middle. Noah Sadiki (#14, Sunderland) — born
December 2004, 21. The generation coming through. Samuel Moutoussamy
(#8, Atromitos) — the holding midfielder.

Attack: Yoane Wissa (#20, Newcastle United) — THE equalizer vs
Portugal, 45+5'. The story of the match. Was at Brentford for years,
now at Newcastle. Composed, clinical, the right man for the big
moment. Cedric Bakambu (#17, Real Betis) — the veteran FW, born
1991. Gael Kakuta (#11, AEL) — former Chelsea prospect, 35 now,
still contributing. Meschak Elia (#13, Alanyaspor) — the pace option.
Theo Bongonda (#10, Spartak Moscow) — the creative #10.

French-Belgian DNA: Wan-Bissaka, Mbemba, Masuaku, Tuanzebe, Mukau,
Sadiki — half the squad playing in France, England, Belgium.

## Group K Results
- POR 1-1 COD (Matchday 1) — Portugal's Joao Neves scored early at
  6'. DR Congo absorbed pressure, stayed organized, then Wissa struck
  at 45+5' to equalize. A draw against one of Europe's strongest
  squads. Desabre's defensive discipline held for long enough, and
  Wissa's composure did the rest.

1 pt. Earned. Against Portugal. That matters.

## Storylines
- Wissa's moment: Yoane Wissa, who spent years at Brentford as a
  cult player before moving to Newcastle, equalized against Portugal
  in the 50th minute of added first-half time. One touch, one finish.
  The Congolese diaspora's best night in 52 years.
- Wan-Bissaka's international choice: Aaron Wan-Bissaka, whose
  English football career included five years at Manchester United,
  chose DR Congo. Now at a World Cup representing the nation of his
  heritage. The dual-nationality story common to this squad.
- Desabre's African career: a French coach who made African football
  his life's work — Uganda, Egypt, now DR Congo. The opposite of the
  European parachute appointment narrative.
- The 1974 shadow: Zaire's World Cup was iconic for all the wrong
  reasons. Five decades later, a draw with Portugal on the biggest
  stage is the beginning of a different story.
- Mukau and Sadiki's youth: two 21-year-olds (both born 2004) in
  central midfield positions. The future of Congolese football is
  already here.

## Observed Patterns
- Defensive compactness allowing limited Portugal chances from open
  play despite territorial disadvantage
- Transition speed through Wissa, Elia, and Bongonda
- Diaspora squad — tactical familiarity with European playing styles
  makes them hard to surprise
- Clinical finishing on limited chances (1 goal from tight opening
  across first half)
- Vulnerability to sustained high pressing not yet fully tested
