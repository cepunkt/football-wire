# Croatia (CRO)

## Tournament History
7th World Cup appearance as independent Croatia (1998, 2002, 2006,
2014, 2018, 2022, 2026). 3rd place 1998 (Suker Golden Boot). Runners-
up 2018 (Dalic's miracle run, lost to France 4-2 in the final). 3rd
place again 2022. The most consistent overachiever relative to
population — four million people, one final, two bronze medals.
Modric's generation is aging out. Is this the last time?

## Current Squad
Coach: Zlatko Dalic (CRO). The man who took over in crisis in 2017
and took Croatia to the 2018 final. Still here. 2022 third place.
Now navigating the transition from the golden generation to whatever
comes next. The most successful coach in Croatian history.
Assistants: Ivica Olic (CRO — former Bayern Munich, 103 caps),
Vedran Corluka (CRO — former Tottenham, Man City, 103 caps). The
entire coaching staff are Croatian football royalty.

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. Built around midfield control. Modric
organizes, Kovacic carries, Vlasic creates. The system serves the
talent, not the other way around.

GK: Dominik Livakovic (#1, Dinamo Zagreb) — the 2022 penalty
specialist. Saved multiple penalties at Qatar. 31, returned to Dinamo
Zagreb. Still the first choice.

Defence: Josko Gvardiol (#4, Man City) — born January 2002, 24.
The best young CB in world football two years ago. The obvious future.
The transition's brightest light. Marin Pongraci (#3, Fiorentina) —
the partner. Josip Stanisic (#2, Bayern) — born 2000, right-back.
Luka Vuskovic (#22, Hamburger SV) — born February 2007, 19. At
Hamburger SV. The youngest of the generation arriving.

Midfield: Luka Modric (#10, AC Milan) — born September 1985, 40
years old. At AC Milan now. STILL THERE. The greatest Croatian player
in history, the 2018 Ballon d'Or, the Real Madrid legend. At 40, this
is clearly the last World Cup. Mateo Kovacic (#8, Man City) — the
engine, the Modric heir. Martin Baturina (#16, Como) — born February
2003, 22. SCORED vs England at 36'. The young star at Como. Petar
Sucic (#17, Inter Milan) — born October 2003, 22. Inter Milan. The
next midfielder.

Attack: Andrej Kramaric (#9, Hoffenheim) — the veteran striker,
born 1991. Still Croatia's most reliable finisher. Ivan Perisic (#14,
PSV) — born 1989, 37. The left wing legend, now at PSV. Ante
Budimir (#11, Osasuna) — physical option in the penalty area.
Petar Musa (#26, FC Dallas) — SCORED vs England at 45+5'. The MLS-
based forward who scored in stoppage time to briefly make it 2-2 at
half time. Born 1998.

The Pasalic brothers: Mario Pasalic (#15, Atalanta) and Marco
Pasalic (#24, Orlando City SC) — brothers, both in the squad.

  half time. Then England's second half took over: Bellingham 47',

## Storylines
- Modric at 40: is this finally the end? AC Milan, 40 years old,
  still there in the squad, still wearing #10. The 2018 Ballon d'Or
  winner's last World Cup is a certainty by now. Whether he can
  produce one more magical performance before the curtain falls is
  the only question left.
- Baturina's emergence: the 22-year-old Como midfielder scored
  against England, in a World Cup, in his first major tournament.
  At the same age Modric was just beginning at Dinamo Zagreb. The
  torch is being lit, slowly.
- The 2-2 half-time ghost: Croatia fought back from 1-0 to 2-2 at
  half time through Baturina and Musa. The second half told a
  different story — Bellingham's third sealed it. The moments Croatia
  had to believe they could rescue a point made the collapse more
  painful.
- Gvardiol's generational weight: at 24, Gvardiol is meant to be the
  foundation of Croatian football for the next decade. He carries
  Dalic's hopes for what comes after Modric.
- Corluka and Olic on the bench: 103 caps each, European club careers.
  Croatian football royalty watching the golden generation's last stand
  from the dugout.

## Observed Patterns
- First-half competitiveness that fades in the second — physical
  intensity not sustained over 90'
- Baturina and Sucic showing genuine quality but raw at this level
- Modric's influence is gravitational even at 40 — opponents still
  must account for him
- Livakovic reliable in goal but two conceded from set pieces and
  open play suggests defensive exposure
- Counter-attacking threat real but requires defensive stability
  that England denied
