# Canada (CAN)

## Tournament History
3rd World Cup appearance (1986, 2022, 2026). In 1986 they lost all
three without scoring. In 2022 they returned after 36 years, lost
all three again but scored their first-ever WC goal (Davies vs
Belgium). Now as 2026 co-hosts, the third time delivers: first ever
WC point (1-1 vs BIH) and first ever WC win (6-0 vs QAT, David
hat trick).

## Current Squad
Coach: Jesse Marsch (USA). American coach leading Canada — an unusual
pairing that reflects how far MLS culture and CONCACAF development
have converged. Tactically flexible, high-energy pressing style.
Assistants: Paul Stalteri (CAN — former international, 87 caps),
Nicolas Gagnon (CAN), Ewan Sharp (CAN), Mauro Biello (CAN — former
MLS Cup winner as player).

System: 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1. High press, vertical transitions, exploiting
Alphonso Davies' width and Jonathan David's movement in behind.

GK: Dayne St. Clair (#1, Inter Miami CF) — the starting keeper.
Maxime Crepeau (#16, Orlando City) backup.

Key axis: Alphonso Davies (#19, Bayern Munich) — the left flank
destroyer, 25 years old, one of the world's best fullbacks. Jonathan
David (#10, Juventus) — Canada's all-time top scorer, hat trick vs
Qatar, clinical finisher. Cyle Larin (#9, Southampton) — physical
striker, all-time international goals leader, scored in both group
matches. Stephen Eustaquio (#7, LAFC) — the midfield engine.

Depth: Ismail Kone (#8, Sassuolo), Liam Millar (#11, Hull City),
Tajon Buchanan (#17, Villarreal — injured in 2024 but returning).
Tani Oluwaseyi (#12, Villarreal) adds pace off the bench.

Young guns: Luc de Fougerolles (#4, FCV Dender, born 2005-10-12),
Nathan Saliba (#25, Anderlecht, born 2004-02-07), Niko Sigur (#23,
Hajduk Split, born 2003-09-09). Jonathan Osorio (#21, Toronto FC) is
the oldest outfield player (born 1992) — a local hero at home.

## Group B Results
- CAN 1-1 BIH (Matchday 1) — Larin 78' / Lukic 21'. Canada came from
  behind against Bosnia. Larin equalized late after BIH opened through
  Lukic in the first half. Frustrated start for the co-hosts.
- CAN 6-0 QAT (Matchday 2) — Larin 16', David 29' 45+3' 90+2',
  Saliba 64', Manai OG 75'. Emphatic response. David's hat trick
  (three different finishes) was the headline but Saliba's goal from
  midfield and the team's pressing intensity was the story. 7 goals
  in 2 matches, 4 pts. Top of the group on goal difference.

Qualified with 4 pts alongside Switzerland. MD3 vs SUI decides group
winners.

## Storylines
- The Golden Generation comes home: Davies, David, Larin — the trio
  that qualified Canada for the first time in 36 years now plays a
  World Cup on Canadian soil. The weight of a nation in every match.
- Jonathan David's hat trick: three goals in 45 minutes vs Qatar, each
  one different — a composed finish, a header, a clinical run. He is
  now Canada's most dangerous attacker at a World Cup by distance.
- Jesse Marsch, American coach: Canada going to the World Cup with an
  American head coach is either ironic or perfectly CONCACAF. Marsch
  brings MLS knowledge and the high-press Red Bull philosophy. The
  results so far justify the hire.
- The 1-1 vs Bosnia: before the 6-0 demolition, Canada drew 1-1 with
  Bosnia after going behind. Lukic's 21st-minute opener forced a
  reaction. Larin came off the bench to level. The response to
  adversity mattered more than the result.
- MD3 vs Switzerland: four points apiece, GD +6 vs +3. Canada need
  only a draw to top the group. Playing in front of a home crowd
  against a Switzerland side that has never beaten them in major
  tournament football.

## Observed Patterns
- Devastating on the counter and in transition — the 6-0 vs Qatar
  showcased how quickly they can break from a press
- Davies as attacking fullback creates 2v1 down the left constantly;
  opposition must commit extra coverage or concede space
- David's movement off the shoulder of the last defender is elite
  — he has scored three different types of goal in two matches
- Set piece vulnerability: BIH's 21st-minute opener came from a
  structured play; Canada's defensive organization from dead balls
  needs attention
- Depth in midfield is MLS-heavy — Eustaquio and Kone are solid but
  the quality drop is noticeable when either is off the ball
