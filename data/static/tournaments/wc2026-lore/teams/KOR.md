# South Korea (KOR)

## Tournament History
11th consecutive World Cup appearance (1986-2026). The streak. Best:
4th place in 2002 (co-hosts). 2022: group stage exit. The 2002
generation — Park Ji-sung, Ahn Jung-hwan — set expectations that
every Korean team since has been measured against. Hong Myung-bo was
the captain of that 2002 team. Now he's the coach.

## Current Squad
Coach: Hong Myung-bo (KOR). 2002 World Cup hero, captain of the
semi-final team. Appointed 2024. Returning to finish what he started
as a player 24 years ago. Controversial appointment — fan pushback
over his earlier stint (2014).
Assistants: Lee Jaehong, Kim Dong-Jin, Joao Aroso (POR),
Kim Jin-Kyu, Nuno Matias (POR). Two Portuguese coaches in the staff
— Iberian tactical influence.

System: 4-2-3-1 / 4-3-3. Built around Son as the focal point.
Organized defensive structure, quick transitions through Son and
Lee Kang-in's creativity. Hong demands disciplined shape and
fast vertical play.

Key players: Son Heung-min (#7, FW) LAFC — the captain, the star,
South Korea's all-time top scorer. Moved from Tottenham to LAFC.
At 33, this is likely his last World Cup. Everything runs through
him. Kim Min-jae (#4, DF) Bayern Munich — world class centre-back.
The "Monster." Lee Kang-in (#19, MF) PSG — the creative engine,
25 years old, one of the most gifted Korean players ever.
Hwang In-beom (#6, MF) Feyenoord — scored against CZE, box-to-box
energy.

Generation bridge: Son (33), Kim Seung-gyu (35) represent the
experienced core. Bae Jun-ho (#17, Stoke, 22), Yang Hyun-jun (#20,
Celtic, 24), Jens Castrop (#23, Monchengladbach, 22) represent the
future.

Castrop (#23) — German-Korean, plays in the Bundesliga. An
interesting dual heritage selection.

## Group A Results
- KOR 2-1 CZE (Matchday 1) — Krejci 59' for CZE, then Hwang In-beom
  67' and Oh Hyeon-gyu 80' completed the comeback. All goals in the
  second half. Son's leadership visible in the comeback.
- MEX 1-0 KOR (Matchday 2) — Romo 50'. Narrow loss at the Azteca.
  Korea competed but couldn't break down Mexico's defensive structure
  or overcome the home advantage.

3 pts. MD3 vs RSA will determine qualification.

## Storylines
- Hong Myung-bo's return: the 2002 captain managing at a World Cup.
  24 years between the semi-final and the coaching bench. If Korea
  advance, it completes a narrative arc spanning a generation.
- Son's last World Cup: at 33, moved to MLS, this is almost
  certainly the final tournament. The greatest Korean footballer
  ever, on the biggest stage, one last time.
- Lee Kang-in's emergence: PSG's playmaker is the present and future
  of Korean football. If Son is the heart, Lee is the brain.
- The 2-1 comeback against CZE: falling behind and winning shows
  resilience and squad depth. Oh Hyeon-gyu's 80' winner was a
  classic poacher's goal.
- Kim Min-jae as the defensive foundation: Bayern Munich's centre-back
  anchoring the Korean defence. Arguably the best Asian defender ever.

## Observed Patterns
- Second half team: both goals vs CZE came after 65'. The team
  grows into matches.
- Vulnerable to quick goals: conceded at 50' vs MEX, needed time to
  adjust.
- Son as the transition trigger: when Korea break, Son is the target.
  His movement creates space for Lee and Hwang.
- Compact defensive shape but can be carved open by patient build-up
  (Mexico demonstrated this)
- Set piece threat through Hwang's delivery and Kim Min-jae's aerial
  presence
