# MEX 2-0 RSA — Matchday 1, Group A (Press Report)

## Result
Mexico 2-0 South Africa | Estadio Azteca, Mexico City | 2026-06-11
Attendance: 80,824 | Opening match of FIFA World Cup 2026

## The Tournament's First Goal
Julian Quinones scored the first goal of the 2026 World Cup in the
9th minute. Erik Lira pressed Sithole into an error on the edge of
the box and Quinones drove a low shot through goalkeeper Ronwen
Williams's legs. Only the second player after Pele to score a World
Cup's opening tournament goal for the hosts.

## Jimenez's Moment
Raul Jimenez headed in from Alvarado's cross at the far post in the
67th minute — his first ever World Cup goal at age 35. Visibly
emotional, he dedicated it to his recently deceased father. The
comeback narrative is extraordinary: severe skull fracture in November
2020 that nearly ended his career. Five and a half years later,
scoring at a World Cup in the Azteca. The press called it the
tournament's first great human story.

## Three Red Cards — A World Cup Record
South Africa were reduced to nine men through three red cards, a
record for any World Cup opening match:
- Sithole 49' — denial of an obvious goal-scoring opportunity
- Zwane 82' — violent conduct (VAR review)
- Montes 90'+2' — cynical foul

Hugo Broos's game plan was effectively destroyed by the first red
card. South Africa had competed for the entire first half despite
going behind early, but 11v10 from the 49th minute made it a
containment exercise that eventually collapsed.

## Press Reaction

**Tactical assessment**: Javier Aguirre's Mexico were efficient rather
than spectacular. Erik Lira rated 8/10 across multiple outlets for
pressing intensity — his pressure created the first goal. Quinones
8/10 for directness and finishing. Mexico controlled without
dominating; the Azteca did half the work through sheer intimidation.

**Atmosphere**: Described as electric across all sources. 80,824 at
capacity, "Ole!" chants from the first minutes. Broos had warned
his team about the Azteca atmosphere before the match. A USA flag
was booed during the pre-match parade of nations — the co-host
relationship has edges.

**Aguirre post-match**: admitted he was "nervous" and wants more
confidence from his side. Pragmatic as ever — the result mattered
more than the performance. Rafael Marquez (assistant) seen giving
tactical instructions throughout.

**Broos post-match**: defended his players' effort before the red
cards. Pointed to the first half as evidence South Africa could
compete at this level. The disciplinary collapse overshadowed what
was a competitive opening 49 minutes.

**Jimenez narrative**: dominated press coverage. The skull fracture,
the rehabilitation, the father's death, the World Cup goal at 35.
Multiple outlets ran feature pieces within hours. "The most emotional
moment of the opening day" (ESPN). "Football's greatest comeback
story reaches its climax" (Goal.com).

## Wider Context
- Mexico host matches at Azteca, Estadio Akron (Guadalajara), and
  Estadio BBVA (Monterrey). The home advantage is real — altitude
  (2,240m in Mexico City), crowd, familiarity.
- South Africa's three reds will trigger suspensions across Group A.
  Sithole, Zwane, and Montes all miss the Czech Republic match.
- Quinones playing at the Azteca for the host nation — he plays for
  Club America domestically. Literally his home pitch.
