## Stakes
- **JPN win**: moves to 4 pts, level with NED. Would guarantee at
  least 3rd place and likely a top-2 finish depending on MD3 vs SWE.
  Could even top the group with a good MD3 result.
- **TUN win**: moves to 3 pts, level with SWE. Would need MD3 result
  vs NED, but at least alive. GD still a problem (-4 to overcome).
- **Draw**: JPN on 2 pts, TUN on 1 pt. Both still alive but relying
  on MD3. Japan in better position with SWE to play. Tunisia would
  face NED — brutal.

## The Renard Factor
This is Herve Renard's first match in charge. Appointed 6 days ago
after Lamouchi's sacking. He had the Saudi Arabia squad for months
before beating Argentina in 2022 — here he's had less than a week.

But Renard's DNA is clear: defensive organization, compact shape,
counter-attacking on transitions, maximum effort for 90 minutes. If
anyone can impose a structure in 48 hours of preparation, it's him.

Expect a transformed defensive setup from the 5-1 shambles against
Sweden. The question is whether he's had time to fix the structural
problems or just patch over them.

## Key Matchups
- **Kubo (#8 JPN) vs Rekik/Bronn**: Kubo drifts inside from the
  right, Tunisia's centre-backs will need to track his movement.
  If Kubo finds space between the lines, Japan's creative threat
  multiplies.
- **Kamada (#15 JPN) vs Skhiri (#17 TUN)**: Kamada scored the late
  equalizer against NED. Skhiri is Tunisia's most experienced
  midfielder (Frankfurt teammate of Kamada in 2022-23). They know
  each other's game.
- **Japan's set pieces vs Tunisia's aerial presence**: Japan were
  vulnerable to Van Dijk's header against NED. Tunisia's best hope
  may be from set pieces — Rekik already scored from one vs Sweden.
- **Renard's organization vs Moriyasu's flexibility**: Tactical chess
  match between two experienced coaches. Moriyasu showed the ability
  to switch between 3-back and 4-back against NED. Renard will
  prioritize keeping it tight first.

## Head to Head
Limited history. Different tiers of Asian/African football. No major
tournament meetings in recent memory. Japan are heavy favourites on
squad quality and current form.

## Venue
Estadio BBVA, Monterrey (Guadalupe). Home of CF Monterrey. Modern
stadium, artificial surface concerns possible but confirmed natural
grass for WC. Northern Mexico summer — hot and humid for a night
kick-off but more manageable than daytime.

## Prediction Territory (campfire)
Japan should win this. They were excellent against the Netherlands and
have far more European-league quality across the pitch. But Renard is
the wildcard — he specializes in exactly this situation: underdog,
written off, low expectations. Japan 2-0 or 2-1 feels right. But if
Renard has found a way to shore up the defence and Tunisia hit Japan
on the counter or from set pieces, a draw or even an upset isn't
impossible. History says Renard in a World Cup is dangerous.
