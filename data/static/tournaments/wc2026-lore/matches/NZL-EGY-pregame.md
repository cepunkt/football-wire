# NZL vs EGY — Matchday 2, Group G (Pre-Game)

## Match Info
New Zealand vs Egypt | Vancouver, BC Place | 2026-06-22 01:00 UTC
Capacity 54,000. Retractable roof. Pacific time zone.

## Group G State (after Matchday 1)
All teams level on 1 pt.

## Stakes
Winner takes pole position in the group. Loser faces a must-win MD3.
For Egypt, this is the match they should win — the quality gap
between Salah/Marmoush and NZ's back line is the biggest individual
mismatch in the group.

## Key Matchups
- **Salah (#10 EGY) + Marmoush (#22 EGY) vs NZ's defence**: The
  Liverpool-Man City attacking duo against a defence anchored by
  lower-league English and NZ domestic players. The quality gap is
  enormous. If Egypt's forwards turn up, this could be one-sided.
- **Chris Wood (#9 NZL) vs Abdelmonem (#6 EGY)**: Wood's physical
  aerial presence vs Egypt's Nice-based centre-back. NZ's only
  reliable attacking threat needs service to make an impact.
- **Just (#11 NZL) vs Egypt's full-backs**: Just scored twice vs
  Iran through direct running. If he can repeat that against Egypt's
  wide defenders, NZ have a chance.
- **Abdelkarim (#9 EGY)**: The 18-year-old Barcelona forward. If
  Hossam Hassan gives him minutes, it could be special.

## Prediction Territory (campfire)
Egypt should win this. Salah and Marmoush against NZ's defence is a
significant quality mismatch. But NZ showed against Iran they can
score and compete. Egypt 2-1 or 3-1 feels right. A NZ win would be
one of the results of the tournament.
