# URU vs CPV — Matchday 2, Group H (Pre-Game)

## Match Info
Uruguay vs Cape Verde | Miami, Hard Rock Stadium | 2026-06-21
Capacity 65,000. Open air. South Florida summer — heat and humidity
a significant factor. Sea level.

## Group H State (after Matchday 1)
All teams level on 1 pt.

## Stakes
Uruguay need a win after drawing with Saudi Arabia. Anything less
than 3 points and Bielsa's team face a potential must-win MD3 against
Spain. For Cape Verde, another draw would be extraordinary — 2 points
from Spain and Uruguay would be a historic group stage performance.

## The Bielsa vs the Bus
Bielsa's pressing system was frustrated by Saudi Arabia's compact
block. Cape Verde's entire identity is defensive organization — the
0-0 against Spain proved they can deny space and chances to the
best teams in the world. This is the ultimate test of Bielsa's
philosophy: relentless intensity vs immovable resistance.

Miami's heat adds a dimension: Bielsa's system demands extraordinary
physical output. 30°C+ with humidity will test Uruguay's fitness and
discipline. Does the press survive the conditions?

## Key Matchups
- **Valverde (#8 URU) + Nunez (#9 URU) vs CPV's defensive unit**:
  Uruguay's quality against Cape Verde's organization. Valverde's
  driving runs and Nunez's explosive movement should create chances.
  But Spain had chances too and scored zero.
- **Vozinha (#1 CPV) vs everything Uruguay throw at him**: The 40-year-old
  who denied Spain. If he produces another heroic display, Cape Verde
  could steal another result. But two consecutive clean sheets against
  this quality would be unprecedented.
- **Logan Costa (#5 CPV) vs Nunez**: Villarreal's centre-back is Cape
  Verde's highest-quality defender. He'll be tasked with containing
  Darwin's physical presence and pace.
- **Bielsa's subs**: Uruguay's bench (de Arrascaeta, Pellistri,
  Rodriguez, Aguirre) provides fresh legs. In the Miami heat, the
  team that manages the last 30 minutes better wins.

## Prediction Territory (campfire)
Uruguay should win. Bielsa will have studied the Cape Verde 0-0 and
prepared solutions. The heat might slow the press but Uruguay's
quality in transition — Valverde, Nunez, de la Cruz — should find
spaces. Uruguay 2-0 or 1-0. But if Cape Verde hold firm for 60
minutes, doubt creeps in and Vozinha becomes the story again.
