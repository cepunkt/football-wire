## Alaba vs Messi — The Subplot
David Alaba has a 2-0 career record against Lionel Messi. Real
Madrid vs Barcelona, Champions League — Alaba's tactical intelligence
and positioning have neutralized Messi in big games before. He knows
how to defend against the greatest player ever.

But this isn't Barcelona Messi. This is 38-year-old Inter Miami Messi
(turns 39 on June 24, two days after this match).
He doesn't run past you anymore. He makes the ball do the work. The
hat trick against Algeria showed a player operating in spaces that
defenders haven't covered yet. Alaba's challenge isn't physical — it's
positional. Can he be where Messi will be, before Messi decides
to be there?

## Cordoba 1978
Austria 3-2 West Germany at the 1978 World Cup. Krankl's 88' winner.
Edi Finger's radio commentary — "I WERDE NARRISCH!" The Cordoba
anniversary is today — June 21. 48 years to the day. Tomorrow is
Argentina. Austria at a World Cup, facing a South American giant,
everyone expecting them to lose. The parallels write themselves.

## Tactical Preview
**Rangnick's press vs Scaloni's build-up**: This is the chess match.
Rangnick will press high — Laimer, Seiwald, X. Schlager as the
pressing machines. Can they force turnovers against De Paul, Mac
Allister, Enzo Fernandez? Argentina's midfield quality is elite.
But Rangnick's system is designed for exactly this: high turnovers
creating quick chances.

**Key battle**: Austria's press vs Argentina's ability to play through
it. If Argentina can bypass the press (they have the quality), the
space behind Austria's high line is exploitable. Messi in space
behind Danso and Alaba is terrifying.

**The counter-argument**: when Austria's press works — as it did in
patches against Jordan — the turnovers create immediate danger.
Schmid, Wimmer, Sabitzer can all finish. If they win the ball high
against Argentina, the chance quality is there.

## Key Matchups
- **Messi (#10 ARG) vs Alaba (#8 AUT)**: The defining individual
  battle. Alaba's positioning vs Messi's spatial awareness.
- **Laimer (#20 AUT) + Seiwald (#6 AUT) vs De Paul (#7 ARG) +
  Mac Allister (#20 ARG)**: The midfield press. Austria's energy vs
  Argentina's technique.
- **Alvarez (#9 ARG) vs Danso (#3 AUT)**: Physical battle. Alvarez's
  movement vs Danso's aggression. Tottenham CB vs Atletico striker.
- **Sabitzer (#9 AUT) set pieces**: Sabitzer's delivery against
  Argentina's sometimes-vulnerable aerial defence.

## Venue
AT&T Stadium, Dallas. 94,000 capacity. Home of the Dallas Cowboys.
Retractable roof will likely be closed — indoor atmosphere at
impossible scale. The noise will be extraordinary. The Argentine
support in Texas will be massive.

## Prediction Territory (campfire)
Argentina should win. They have Messi, they have the system, they
have the 2022 experience. But Austria under Rangnick are not a team
that lies down. The press will create uncomfortable moments.
Argentina 2-1 or 2-0 is the likely outcome. But if Austria score
first and Austria's press stays energized, a draw or even an upset
is not fantasy. Alaba knows how to beat Messi. The question is
whether the team around him can sustain it for 90 minutes.

Austria haven't beaten Argentina since... well, since 1978. In
Cordoba. Sometimes history rhymes.
