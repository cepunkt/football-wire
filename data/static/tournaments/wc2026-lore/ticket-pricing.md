# The Ticket Pricing Crisis
> Developing story — updated as the tournament progresses

---

## The Numbers

Face-value ticket prices, 2026 World Cup (USD, before 15% service fee):

| Stage | Cheapest | Most expensive |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Group stage | $380 | $4,105 |
| Semi-final | $2,705 | $11,130 |
| Final | $2,030 | $32,970 |

A nominal $60 "Supporter Entry Tier" exists but is distributed
through national federations, not public sale. Effectively invisible.

## Compared to Qatar 2022

| | Qatar 2022 | USA 2026 | Increase |
|---|-----------|----------|----------|
| Cheapest group stage | $11 | $380 | ~35x |
| Cheapest final | $206 | $2,030 | ~10x |
| Most expensive final | ~$1,600 | $32,970 | ~20x |

## Dynamic Pricing

FIFA used demand-based variable pricing. The Final's best seats
were tripled from $10,990 to $32,970 in May 2026 — one month before
the tournament. Economists flagged suspicious bulk inventory transfers
to secondary platforms below official prices.

FIFA runs its own official resale platform and takes 30% of every
secondary transaction.

## Resale Market

Over half of remaining matches have a resale get-in price above
$1,000. Final resale minimum: $8,303. After opening matches, several
fixtures spiked 100-136% on secondary sites. The market is designed
to extract maximum value at every stage.

## Fan Reaction

- England's official allocation for the Final failed to sell out.
  Cheapest FA ticket: £3,119. At Euro 2024: £83.
- 180,000 unsold tickets before the tournament opened.
- Football Supporters Europe filed a complaint with the European
  Commission.
- Joint attorney general investigation in New York and New Jersey
  into pricing practices.
- FIFA eventually slashed prices across matches and returned 70%
  of block-booked hotel rooms.

## The Pattern

Same mechanism as the hydration breaks: FIFA uses the tournament's
scale to extract maximum revenue while marketing "accessibility"
and "the people's game." The $60 tier exists for the press release.
The $380 floor exists for the fans. The $32,970 ceiling exists for
the clients FIFA is actually building for.

The billions watch on streams that buffer. The few who make billions
sit in Category 1. Football's economics laid bare: the product is
the audience, not the spectator.

## Developing

Price slashing and unsold inventory suggest the ceiling was set too
high even for the target market. The attorney general investigation
and European Commission complaint could produce findings during or
after the tournament.
